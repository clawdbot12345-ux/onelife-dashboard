# International Benchmarks — Health & Supplement Brands Teardown

**Phase 1A research artifact for the One Life Growth Engine**
Prepared for One Life Health (4 stores in Gauteng + onelife.co.za on Shopify; own-brand Vivid Health).
Research date: 2026-06-12. Web search and fetch **were available and used**; source URLs are cited inline.

> Scope note: This teardown reads public marketing surfaces (loyalty pages, help centres, ad-strategy teardowns, press, benchmark reports). Internal flow logic and exact email cadences for private brands are inferred from public artifacts and third-party teardowns, and are flagged as inference where relevant. Use this as a strategy input, not a compliance authority — the SA regulatory section is directional, confirm with a SAHPRA/ASA specialist before publishing claims.

## 1. Holland & Barrett (UK retail — closest analogue to One Life)

- **Business model:** Multi-channel health retailer — ~700+ stores plus web and app, private-label heavy (own brands drive margin), supported by in-store advisor expertise. The most directly comparable model to One Life: bricks-and-mortar health specialist + e-commerce + own label.
- **Loyalty (the headline lesson):** Relaunched its scheme in Feb 2026 as **H&B&Me**, merging its separate lifestyle app and the old "Rewards for Life" card into one programme. Members earn 4 points per £1, redeemable as money-off — but the relaunch's key shift was **instant point spend in-store or online** (no waiting for monthly vouchers), plus **Member Prices** (members-only pricing tier), a **free birthday treat**, and tailored offers. ([H&B&Me](https://www.hollandandbarrett.com/info/rewards-for-life/), [The Grocer](https://www.thegrocer.co.uk/news/holland-and-barrett-to-relaunch-membership-scheme-in-new-loyalty-push/714173.article), [Marketing Week](https://www.marketingweek.com/holland-barrett-launches-its-first-customer-loyalty-scheme/))
- **Email/CRM:** Loyalty data feeds segmentation — emails are tailored to shopper habits, and a transactional trigger fires "you have points ready to spend." Health-advice content emails are the acquisition hook at signup. ([Essential Retail interview](https://www.essentialretail.com/interviews/holland-barrett-loyalty-data/))
- **In-store ↔ online integration:** Single ID earns and burns across store/web/app — the unified-wallet model. This is the integration pattern One Life should copy.
- **Compliance:** Operates under UK/EU nutrition & health claims regime; product pages lean on "supports/contributes to" function language rather than disease claims.

## 2. AG1 / Athletic Greens (single-SKU DTC — the category-creation masterclass)

- **Business model:** One product, subscription-first. ~$600M annual revenue / $1.2B valuation reportedly off effectively a single SKU. ([Optimonk](https://www.optimonk.com/athletic-greens-marketing-breakdown), [Marketer.club](https://www.marketer.club/p/athletic-greens-ag1-built-1b-brand-with-only-one-product))
- **Hero-product strategy:** Refused to call it a "greens powder" — invented the category **"Foundational Nutrition"** with one ritual: "one scoop a day, you've covered your bases." Category creation removes price comparison. ([Optimonk](https://www.optimonk.com/athletic-greens-marketing-breakdown))
- **Email/subscription mechanics:** Subscription is the default purchase path (monthly auto-ship); the **Welcome Kit funnel reportedly carries ~60% of paid volume** — a free-gift-with-subscription offer (shaker, vitamin D drops, travel packs) that makes the subscription feel like a deal, not a lock-in. ([Influee teardown](https://influee.co/landing/ag1-meta-ads-teardown))
- **Content/paid engine:** Third-largest podcast sponsor (after BetterHelp & Manscaped), ~$2.2M/month on podcasts; 20% affiliate commission; an "Authority Shield" stacking experts (Huberman), clinical references, and certifications. Creative is dominated by UGC variants: testimonial+demo, unboxing, "yapper" talking-head, comment-response, expert explainer — creator-native per market. ([Latterly](https://www.latterly.org/ag1-marketing-strategy/), [Sandstorm](https://sandstormapp.com/blog/ag1-athletic-greens-marketing-strategy), [Motion library](https://motionapp.com/library/ag1))
- **Compliance:** Wellness/structure-function framing ("energy, gut health, immune support"), avoids disease claims; leans on third-party expert credibility rather than its own disease claims.

## 3. Ritual (transparency-led DTC subscription)

- **Business model:** DTC subscription multivitamin, ~$250M revenue. Daily-consumption product = natural subscription fit; stable MRR. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/03/07/how-ritual-founder-katerina-schneider-built-a-successful-vitamin-brand.html))
- **Hero-product strategy:** "Essential for Women" multivitamin as the wedge, then expanded by life-stage (prenatal, 50+, men's, kids). **Radical transparency** is the differentiator: clear capsule, named ingredient sources, linked studies, "The future of vitamins is clear." Earned **USP Verification** and ran its own clinical trials on the finished formula — rare in supplements. ([Pati Group](https://www.patigroup.com/p/ritual-disrupting-the-supplement), [Beauty Independent](https://www.beautyindependent.com/dtc-vitamin-ritual-retail-advocacy-sustainability-ingredient-traceability/))
- **Email/lifecycle:** Transparency + education + lifecycle marketing across organic, paid, and email; subscription incentive is **25% off first shipment, settling to ~15%** thereafter. ([Pati Group](https://www.patigroup.com/p/ritual-disrupting-the-supplement), [DTC Patterns](https://www.dtcpatterns.com/dtc-patterns-articles/get-better-gut-health-daily-with-rituals-subscription-program))
- **Content engine:** Editorial built on "morning ritual," self-empowerment, ingredient traceability storytelling; publishes studies publicly.
- **Compliance:** Substantiation-first — clinical trials and USP verification are the marketing asset, neutralising the "supplements are snake oil" objection.

## 4. iHerb (global marketplace retailer)

- **Business model:** Cross-border e-commerce marketplace shipping to 180+ countries; competes on price + selection + fast global logistics (US, South Korea, Hong Kong DCs). Relevant to One Life as the *price/selection benchmark* SA customers compare against. ([RetailMBA](https://www.retailmba.com/iherb-vendor/), [PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/iherb-simplifies-its-rewards-program-300171663.html))
- **Loyalty / growth loop:** Rewards is essentially a **referral engine** — $5 on a referred first order + a recurring 5% of referred customers' order totals, paid as credit. Growth is customer-acquires-customer, not points-for-self. ([iHerb Rewards](https://information.iherb.com/hc/en-us/articles/6692985944724-About-the-iHerb-Rewards-Program))
- **Influencer/affiliate:** Large affiliate + influencer programmes paying % of sales — the distributed-acquisition model. ([iHerb affiliates](https://www.iherb.com/info/affiliates))
- **Free-shipping threshold** (orders over ~$30–40) as the AOV lever.

## 5. Thorne (practitioner channel → omnichannel — the B2B2C lesson)

- **Business model:** 40-year science brand, originally practitioner-only, now omnichannel (own site, Amazon since 2015, Target.com, Mayo Clinic Store). DTC grew **63% YoY**; customers ~4M → ~7M. ([Modern Retail](https://www.modernretail.co/marketing/how-thorne-drove-63-growth-in-dtc-sales-after-a-brand-awareness-push/))
- **Practitioner channel (the moat):** Network of **47,000+ practitioners** — described as a "high-trust, high-retention environment where patients comply with regimens, see results, become repeat customers." Backed by clinical education (The Practitioner's Edge webinars). ([NutraIngredients](https://www.nutraingredients.com/Article/2025/11/03/supplements-in-the-health-care-practitioner-channel-a-gate-kept-growth-area/))
- **Personalisation:** At-home testing → personalised regimens; philosophy "meet them on their journey, on their terms." ([Citeline](https://insights.citeline.com/hbw-insight/wellness/business/strategy/thornes-picture-of-dietary-supplement-personalization-meet-them-on-their-journey-VHAGBB7VEZGCJFKELWM4W7M6IY/))
- **Why it matters for One Life:** A premium independent's equivalent of the practitioner channel is its **in-store advisors and any allied health network** — the highest-trust, highest-retention acquisition surface that pure-DTC brands have to manufacture artificially.

## 6. Momentous (athlete-trust, certification-led)

- **Business model:** "Best-in-class human performance" DTC, founded 2018; deliberately narrow range — calls itself the "anti-supplement supplement company." ([livemomentous.com](https://www.livemomentous.com/), [Athletic Insight](https://www.athleticinsight.com/supplements/momentous-review))
- **Hero/trust strategy:** **NSF Certified for Sport** on every batch (tested for 270+ banned substances) — the certification *is* the positioning. Used by 150+ pro/NCAA teams and 100% of NFL locker rooms. ([Masculine Synergy](https://masculinesynergy.com/momentous-supplements-review/))
- **Influencer-as-cofounder:** Multi-year Huberman partnership — he's a scientific advisor and co-develops products ("Huberman stack" of Tyrosine, L-Theanine etc.), so the endorsement is structural, not a one-off ad. ([PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/momentous-announces-a-multi-year-partnership-with-the-huberman-lab-podcast-and-dr-andrew-huberman-as-a-scientific-advisor-301532106.html))
- **Compliance:** Radical sourcing/testing transparency ("The Momentous Standard") substitutes for hype.

## 7. Huel (meal-replacement, content-and-community)

- **Business model:** Nutritionally-complete food (powders + ready-to-drink), subscription-led DTC scaling into retail. ([Panoramata](https://www.panoramata.co/marketing-strategy-brand/huel))
- **Hero product:** Single nutritional promise ("all 27 essential vitamins + macros"), heroing the RTD bottle in creative — logo/packaging always visible. ([Tribe case study](https://www.tribegroup.co/case-study/huel))
- **Content/creator engine:** Launches and flavour drops via creator swarms (e.g., 28 creators reaching ~739k, ~3.07% engagement); UGC asks for "natural, fun, creative" product placement, not polished ads. ([Tribe](https://www.tribegroup.co/case-study/huel), [Disrupt](https://disruptmarketing.co/case_study/huel/))
- **Email:** Strong deliverability discipline (low spam score ~-1.8) and a recognisable, emoji-forward, high-energy voice (💪💥✨) — brand personality carried into the inbox. ([Panoramata](https://www.panoramata.co/marketing-strategy-brand/huel))

## 8. Myprotein (UK mass sports nutrition — distributed acquisition)

- **Business model:** High-volume, frequent-promo sports nutrition; competes on price + flavour breadth + relentless discounting.
- **Acquisition engine:** Layered creator/affiliate machine — pro athletes (Adesanya, Morsia), reality/lifestyle crossovers (Molly-Mae), viral TikTok (Terry Crews), and a 30-part UGC series. Affiliate commission ~8–10%, 30-day cookie; tiered ambassador scheme (5,000+ followers entry) with product allowance, unique codes, monthly briefs/newsletters. ([Nowfluence](https://www.nowfluence.co/research/MyProteinsInfluencerStrategy), [Lasso](https://getlasso.co/affiliate/myprotein-us/), [Myprotein affiliates](https://us.myprotein.com/c/about-us/ways-to-work-with-us/affiliated-partners/))
- **Lesson for One Life:** The *structured ambassador ladder* (clear entry bar → product + unique code → monthly content brief) is directly clonable for local SA fitness/wellness micro-influencers.

## 9. GNC (US specialty retail — subscription + loyalty + omni)

- **Business model:** Specialty supplement retail (stores + web + Amazon), expanding into health services. ([My Total Retail](https://www.mytotalretail.com/podcast/total-retail-talks/gnc-expands-into-health-services-grows-loyalty-program/))
- **Loyalty + subscription tie:** **myGNC Rewards** (free) + **GNC PRO Access** (paid tier). Crucially, **Auto-Deliver & Save requires loyalty membership** — loyalty and subscription are deliberately coupled, and subscriptions can be set up *in-store by an associate* or online, with full self-serve control (pause/skip/cancel/change date). ([GNC Auto-Deliver](https://www.gnc.com/your-order/subscribe-save.html), [GNC loyalty FAQ](https://www.gnc.com/gnc-loyalty-faqs.html))
- **Omnichannel:** Same-day delivery via Shipt; voice ordering. ([Chain Store Age](https://chainstoreage.com/gnc-personalizes-health-subscription/))

## 10. The Vitamin Shoppe (US specialty retail — POS-driven clienteling)

- **Business model:** Specialty supplement retail with a strong store-associate experience.
- **In-store ↔ online integration (the standout):** POS upgrade surfaces **order history, loyalty status, and product info the moment a shopper walks in**; fixed in-store iPads let customers self-check **Healthy Awards** status and redeem rewards at the register. This is *clienteling* — the store associate sees the digital profile. ([CX Dive](https://www.customerexperiencedive.com/news/the-vitamin-shoppe-pos-seamless-experiences-omnichannel-scott-devlin/731387/))
- **Lesson for One Life:** A premium independent can replicate this cheaply with a Shopify POS that shows customer history + loyalty balance at the till — high-touch advice + visible profile is the independent's edge over iHerb.

## 11. ATP Science (Australia — education-platform-as-brand)

- **Business model:** 100% Australian-owned and manufactured, vertically integrated (R&D → manufacturing → branding → distribution in-house); "Nature Knows Best," patented hero products, "Kings of Collagen." ([atpscience.com.au](https://atpscience.com.au/en-us), [LinkedIn](https://au.linkedin.com/company/atp-science))
- **Content engine (the lesson):** **"The ATP Project"** — a long-running podcast where hosts break down complex health topics into plain, actionable advice. Education *is* the marketing channel; built on Klaviyo + TikTok. ([fatburnersonly](https://www.fatburnersonly.com.au/content/atp-science-at-fbo-supps))
- **Principles:** Research → Educate → Create. A small, science-credible brand winning on owned educational media — the most replicable model for Vivid Health.

## Reference layer: lifecycle benchmarks & compliance

**Klaviyo flow benchmarks (2025, supplement/e-comm):** Welcome flow avg **$2.65 RPR** (top 10% $21.18, ~10.5% placed-order). Abandoned-cart is the highest performer: **~50.5% open, ~6.25% click, ~3.33% placed order, ~$3.65 RPR**. Best practice: stagger welcome vs. abandoned-cart send days to avoid overlap; personalised, time-boxed discounts in abandoned-cart; A/B test timing and offer. ([Klaviyo abandoned-cart benchmarks](https://www.klaviyo.com/blog/abandoned-cart-benchmarks), [Klaviyo flow benchmarks](https://help.klaviyo.com/hc/en-us/articles/360033669452), [Flowium top-15 flows](https://flowium.com/blog/klaviyo-flows/))

**Compliance — US frame (instructive, not binding in SA):** FDA governs labels; **FTC governs advertising/marketing** and requires every health claim be "truthful, not misleading, and substantiated." Structure/function claims ("supports immune function") are allowed *with* the DSHEA disclaimer ("not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease"); disease claims are not. ([FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/health-products-compliance-guidance), [FDA structure/function claims](https://www.fda.gov/food/nutrition-food-labeling-and-critical-foods/structurefunction-claims))

**Compliance — South Africa (the binding frame for One Life):** Health/food supplements are a sub-category of **Complementary Medicines** under the Medicines and Related Substances Act (Act 101 of 1965), regulated by **SAHPRA** (since 2017). Disease-risk-reduction and treatment/cure claims (explicit or implied) are **prohibited**; claims must be substantiated. SAHPRA publishes advertising guidelines for medicines and health products; the **ASA** governs broader ad standards. Note the regulatory environment has been contested (a 2022 court ruling declared certain complementary-medicine regulations unlawful), so confirm current status before publishing claims. ([SAHPRA Complementary Medicines](https://www.sahpra.org.za/complementary-medicines/), [SAHPRA Advertising Guidelines PDF](https://www.sahpra.org.za/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/SAHPGL-INSP-RC-07-Guidelines-for-Advertisement-of-Medicines-and-Health-Products.pdf), [court ruling](https://www.financialinstitutionslegalsnapshot.com/2022/08/02/court-declares-regulations-on-complementary-medicines-health-supplements-unlawful/))

## The 10 Patterns That Separate the Best From the Rest

1. **One hero, one ritual, one sentence.** AG1 ("one scoop a day"), Ritual, Huel, Momentous all anchor the brand on a single hero SKU with a one-line job-to-be-done, then expand by life-stage — never with a wall of SKUs. Clarity beats catalogue.
2. **Invent the category to escape price comparison.** AG1's "Foundational Nutrition" and Momentous's "anti-supplement company" reframe the product so there's nothing to price-match against. Naming is a margin strategy.
3. **Subscription is the default, sold as a gift not a lock-in.** Daily-consumption products map perfectly to auto-ship. The winners reduce friction with a juicy first-order incentive (Ritual 25%→15%) or a free Welcome Kit (AG1, ~60% of paid volume) rather than nagging for the subscribe toggle.
4. **Couple loyalty and subscription into one identity.** GNC gates Auto-Deliver behind loyalty membership; H&B unified app+card into one instant-spend wallet. One ID that earns and burns everywhere is the retention flywheel.
5. **Substantiation is the marketing asset.** Ritual's clinical trials + USP verification and Momentous's NSF-for-Sport aren't compliance overhead — they're the headline. In a low-trust category, proof outsells hype.
6. **Borrow authority structurally, not as ad-hoc ads.** Momentous made Huberman an advisor/co-developer; AG1 built an "Authority Shield" of named experts. Embedded credibility > rented endorsement.
7. **UGC and product-demo creative dominate paid.** ~44% of top supplement ads are product demos and ~33% UGC; the best brands run dozens of creator-native variants (testimonial, unboxing, talking-head, comment-response) and let single-image winners run for months. Polished hero films underperform.
8. **Education is an owned media channel, not a blog afterthought.** ATP's "The ATP Project" podcast and Ritual's ingredient editorial *are* the funnel. The content teaches a health problem; the product is the resolution.
9. **Structured ambassador ladders scale acquisition cheaply.** Myprotein/iHerb run clear tiers: entry bar → product allowance + unique discount code → monthly content brief + recurring commission. Distributed micro-creators beat one big sponsorship.
10. **The store is a data surface, and clienteling is the independent's edge.** Vitamin Shoppe and H&B surface order history + loyalty status at the till. The thing iHerb can't do is a human advisor who already knows your regimen — winners weaponise that.

## So What for One Life

Concrete, stealable moves for a 4-store premium independent in Gauteng with own-brand Vivid Health.

1. **Make Vivid a one-hero, one-ritual brand.** Pick (or build) a single Vivid daily flagship — a Gauteng-relevant "foundational" daily (e.g. a greens/multi or immunity daily) — and give it a one-line ritual and a category name we own ("Daily Foundations by Vivid"). Don't launch Vivid as 30 SKUs; launch one hero and expand by life-stage later. (Pattern 1–2; AG1/Ritual.)

2. **Vivid subscription with a Welcome Kit, not a discount treadmill.** Default the Vivid hero to monthly auto-ship in Shopify. First box includes a branded Vivid shaker/pill organiser + a printed "how to build your stack" card. Mirror AG1's gift-led subscription and Ritual's 25%→15% first-order incentive rather than permanent discounting that erodes premium positioning. (Pattern 3.)

3. **One Life ID = loyalty + subscription + in-store, on Shopify POS.** Build a single membership ("One Life Circle") that earns and burns instantly across all 4 stores and online, like H&B&Me. Gate Vivid Auto-Deliver behind membership (GNC model). Use Shopify POS so the till shows the customer's order history, loyalty balance, and current subscription the moment they walk in — that's our Vitamin Shoppe clienteling edge over iHerb. (Patterns 4, 10.)

4. **Turn store advisors into a "practitioner channel."** Our highest-trust acquisition surface is the in-store consultant. Capture each advised customer into the One Life ID at the till, attribute their regimen, and trigger a tailored Klaviyo flow ("Your advisor recommended…"). This is Thorne's 47k-practitioner moat, miniaturised — and it's something no online-only competitor can replicate in Gauteng. (Pattern 10; Thorne.)

5. **Launch "The Vivid Project" — owned educational media.** Copy ATP Science: a short-form video + email series (and a simple podcast/Reels cadence) where a credentialed SA nutritionist breaks down one health topic per week (sleep, gut, immunity, training recovery). Education is the funnel; Vivid is the resolution. Distribute on TikTok/Instagram + the Klaviyo list. (Pattern 8.)

6. **Build the Klaviyo flow stack to benchmark.** Minimum: Welcome (target >$2.65 RPR), Abandoned Cart (stagger send days, personalised time-boxed offer, target ~50% open / ~3.3% placed-order), Post-Purchase replenishment timed to a 30-day supply ("running low?"), Winback for lapsed members, and a Subscription-saver flow before churn. (Klaviyo benchmarks above.)

7. **Stand up a structured local ambassador ladder.** Recruit Gauteng fitness/wellness micro-creators (gyms, run clubs, Pilates/CrossFit). Tiered like Myprotein: entry bar (~3–5k local followers) → Vivid product allowance + unique discount code → monthly content brief. UGC product-demo and talking-head clips, not polished ads — and let winning single images run for months in Meta. (Patterns 7, 9.)

8. **Make certification/substantiation the Vivid headline.** Pursue the strongest SA-relevant quality marks available (GMP, third-party testing, COA per batch) and put them on the pack and PDP the way Momentous uses NSF and Ritual uses USP. In a low-trust SA supplement market, "here's the batch test" is our differentiator versus grey-import iHerb product. (Patterns 5–6.)

9. **Lock claims to SAHPRA/ASA from day one.** Use structure/function language ("supports immune function," "contributes to normal energy") and avoid any treatment/cure/disease-risk-reduction claim — explicit or implied — across PDPs, emails, ambassador briefs, and ads. Bake an approved-claims library into the content workflow so ambassadors and email copy can't drift off-script. Confirm current SAHPRA status with a specialist before publishing. (SA compliance section.)

10. **AOV + referral loops.** Add a free-shipping/free-in-store-consult threshold to lift online AOV (iHerb model), and run an iHerb-style referral: member gives R-credit, friend gets first-order incentive, referrer earns recurring credit — customer-acquires-customer growth that suits a tight independent marketing budget. (iHerb.)
