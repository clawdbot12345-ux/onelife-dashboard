## 1. Positioning

**The position no competitor occupies** (per the battle matrix): *premium-curated + physical stores + walk-in expertise + own-brand value*. Dis-Chem/Clicks own price but lose on expertise (and we never name them — red line). Faithful to Nature owns natural-online but has no stores and is weak on practitioner-grade supplements. Chrome owns gym-bro. Nobody owns "the knowledgeable health shop for her".

**Brand promise (one sentence):** *Real advice, premium supplements, and our own Vivid range — from the health shop that knows you by name.*

**Messaging hierarchy** (ICP: women 35–50, upper-class, high-LSM):
1. Expertise & trust — "ask the team", staff faces, behind-the-counter knowledge (Proof pillar)
2. Symptom-first empathy — start from her felt problem (tired by 2pm, can't sleep, hormones, gut), never product-first
3. Vivid value — made for One Life customers, practitioner-grade at 35–40% margin pricing headroom, the smart-buyer choice
4. Local convenience — collect today at Centurion/GVS/Edenvale; free delivery over R400
Compliance frame on everything: SAHPRA structure/function verbs only + disclaimers; no competitor/pharmacy mentions, ever.
