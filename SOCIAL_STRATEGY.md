# Onelife Health — Social Media Strategy & Content Plan

_Prepared 14 June 2026. Organic + paid social (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok). Competitive
audit grounded in live follower data, plus a content engine built off Onelife's Omni
product/condition data. This is the plan — opinionated, not a menu._

---

## The one-line thesis

**You are not behind on social — you are imbalanced.** You've quietly built the best
independent health-retail TikTok in South Africa. The entire job is: **(1) pour fuel on
TikTok, (2) clone that winning engine onto Instagram Reels where you're currently
invisible but your premium buyer lives, (3) harvest Facebook — don't farm it.**

**Read the TikTok column again.** With **3 stores**, Onelife has **~6× the TikTok

**Now the problem — Instagram.** @one_life_health_supermarket has **1,286 posts but only

which almost always means static product/promo images instead of Reels. Meanwhile the

videos already winning on TikTok become Reels.

**Facebook (24K)** is a legacy asset. Organic reach there is largely dead. Treat it as a
community / reviews / events / Facebook-Shop *harvesting* channel — maintain it, don't
invest growth effort in it.

### Three brand-hygiene issues to fix immediately (free, high-impact)
1. **Handle inconsistency kills discoverability:** TikTok `@onelifehealthstore`, Instagram
   `@one_life_health_supermarket`, brand "Onelife Health", domain onelife.co.za. Unify to
   one handle everywhere (target `@onelifehealth` / `@onelifehealthsa`). A customer who
   finds you on one platform can't find you on the others right now.
2. **"Supermarket" undercuts premium positioning.** Your brand is a curated,
   practitioner-led apothecary (5,000+ products, 90+ brands, since 1996). "Supermarket" in
   the page names signals commodity. Rename to "Onelife Health".
3. **Bio = conversion, not decoration.** Every profile needs: what you are (SA's
   apothecary), the 3 store locations, one link (Linktree → shop / stores / WhatsApp /
   bestsellers), and a clear "DM us / chat to a consultant" CTA.

## Part 2 — Positioning & awareness strategy

You cannot out-shout Clicks (485K) or Dis-Chem (310K), and you don't need to. They are
**generic mass pharmacy**. Your share-of-voice play is to **own the premium,
practitioner-led, condition-specific niche on short-form video** — the lane the giants are
too broad to occupy and where misinformation is rampant (so credibility wins).

**Brand promise on social:** _"South Africa's apothecary — real people, real advice, the
supplements that actually work for your condition."_

That promise is also your moat against TikTok health misinformation: the research is clear
that **verified, credentialed, "explain-the-why" creators win.** You have qualified
consultants on the floor. Put them on camera — that is something no faceless e-commerce
competitor can copy.

**Format rule (non-negotiable):** short-form vertical video is the growth engine on all
three platforms. **Stop posting static product images to *grow*** — use static
carousels/Stories only for depth and offers, never as the primary growth bet.

has produced nothing. Quality video > volume.
