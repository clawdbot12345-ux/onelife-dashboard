# Design & Premium UX Audit — 2026-07-02 (post theme publish)

Art-direction critique benchmarked against Aesop/Ritual/Seed/AG1. Full
screenshot evidence captured (95+ shots, session archive). **Overall: 5/10.**

**Verdict:** the information architecture, copy voice (Precious), and
merchandising IP (17 consultant-signed protocols, "Signed off by Precious",
the 404 "Looks like that bottle has expired") are already world-class retail
thinking — better than most premium sites. The visual execution is 3–4 years
behind the copy: "a premium apothecary wearing a discount-pharmacy uniform."
The gap is DISCIPLINE, not structure — 9/10 is genuinely reachable.

## Scorecard
Home 5 · Nav 6 · Collections 4 · PDP 4.5 · Vivid PDP 5.5 · Search 4.5 ·
Cart drawer 6.5 (best component) · Cart page 5.5 · **Checkout 3 (unbranded
Shopify defaults at the money moment)** · Quiz 4.5 (form with emoji, not a
signature experience) · Protocols index 6 / detail 7 (site's best template) ·
Brands/Vivid 5 · Blog 6.5 · Footer 6.5 · Contact/policy 4.5 · **Login 3** ·
404 7 · Rewards 4.5 (promises a widget that never renders).

## The 5 gaps to world-class (ranked by visual impact)
1. **The logo is the ceiling.** Purple-pink scribble figure + kelly-green
   block caps "HEALTH STORE" contradicts "apothecary since 1996" on every
   screen incl. checkout and favicon. Nothing else can read premium past it.
2. **No color discipline.** Declared palette (deep green + cream) is
   beautiful; the site pours ~25 colors over it — 11 pastel protocol tiles,
   4-color tag pills, default link-blue ×69 on home, neon #35d477, emoji
   everywhere. Aesop uses ~3.
3. **Un-art-directed product imagery.** Mixed supplier packshots in white
   boxes on cream; Vivid renders in grey mockup frames. The dark botanical
   category tiles prove the team CAN do it — commercial surfaces never got
   the treatment.
4. **Mobile chrome congestion** (83% of purchases): 4 stacked header bars ≈
   750px before content; 3 fixed layers at the bottom.
5. **Highest-stakes moments unbranded**: checkout, login, the quiz (hero CTA
   of the site), Rewards. Premium brands spend most design budget here.

## Elevation plan
**Quick (CSS/settings, days):** one-accent recolor of tiles/pills; kill blue
links + neon + emoji in system UI; heading-font lock (Cormorant discipline);
mobile chrome diet (stat bar → home only, once ≈ +400px first screen);
predictive-search/quiz contrast; checkout editor branding (green/cream/clean
logo — 30 min, most valuable screen); popup: once per session, exit-intent.
**Medium (components, 2–6 wks):** product card v2 (uniform ratio, one tag,
aligned baselines, hide zero-star rows); PDP buy-column reorder (price →
stock/delivery inline → ONE primary CTA → collapsible disclaimer → single
trust panel — currently 9 boxes before the story); quiz as full-screen
takeover with illustrated options + animated steps; cart drawer polish (real
payment logos, mobile void); Rewards embed fix; contact page cards; motion
pass (hover states, ATC animation, skeletons).
**Heavy (identity/photography, 1–3 mo, highest ceiling):** redraw the
brandmark (serif "One Life", "APOTHECARY · EST. 1996", cream/green/gold) →
header, checkout, favicon, email; owned photography system for top 100
sellers + all Vivid (cream/stone, soft light, botanical props — matching the
existing category tiles); Vivid brand stage (story block, lifestyle
photography, "made in SA" PDP module); protocol pages as flagship editorial
with consultant portrait.

Score today 5/10 → quick+medium ≈ 7.5 → +identity/photography tier ≈ 9+.
