# Onelife Health — Brand Voice & Email Template Style

This is the source of truth for how Onelife emails should look and sound.
Derived from the winning NAD+ & NMN campaign (R1,770 revenue, 35% open, 1.69% click).

## Template structure (do not deviate)

```
[Dark green header: Onelife logo centered, 180px wide]
[White content card with 24px padding]
  [Small gray line: "Preview text: <one-line-teaser>"]
  [H1 in #1B5E20, 25px, short and punchy]
  [Paragraph 1 — 1-2 sentences, setting up the topic]
  [Paragraph 2 — 1-2 sentences, leading to the CTA]
  [Green CTA button: "Read the full guide →"]
  [H2 in #1B5E20, 20px: "Top [category] picks" or similar]
  [Simple product list — each row is:
     - bold green product name (link)
     - line break
     - plain gray description, 1 sentence max]
  [Link at bottom: "Shop [category]" in green, no button]
[Light gray footer with small text:]
  Onelife Health stores: Centurion | Glen Village, Faerie Glen | Edenvale
  Free delivery over R900 (Gauteng) | R1,400 (nationwide)
  Unsubscribe link (same gray color, underlined)
```

## Copy voice

- **Conversational but evidence-first.** Not an influencer tone, not a clinical tone — somewhere between "smart pharmacist friend" and "Consumer Reports for supplements".
- **Short paragraphs.** 1-3 sentences each. Never run-on.
- **No hype words.** Never use "amazing", "incredible", "game-changer", "life-changing", "revolutionary".
- **Honest qualifications.** "The research is still emerging" / "modest effect" / "not for everyone" / "if you've tried X without results".
- **Specific over general.** "500 mg three times per day" beats "high doses". "2 kg over 12 weeks" beats "significant weight loss".
- **No exclamation marks** in body copy. Maybe one in a subject line.

## What NOT to include in emails

- ❌ Fake badges like "BEST VALUE" or "RESEARCH-BACKED DOSE" in product modules
- ❌ Prices shown in the email (they're on the product page — let people click through)
- ❌ Multiple CTAs per section
- ❌ Stock photos of smiling people
- ❌ Countdown timers or urgency tactics for non-urgent products
- ❌ "Limited time only" unless it actually is
- ❌ Lime green (`#7ea93e`) or any other green — only `#1B5E20`

## Subject line style

Good examples (from high-performing campaigns):
- "Is Berberine really 'Nature's Ozempic'? (we read the actual studies)"
- "Taking magnesium? You're probably taking the wrong form"
- "The gut health upgrade nobody's talking about"
- "Time to top up? It's been ~50 days"

Pattern: Question, curiosity gap, or honest claim. Never "SALE" or "LIMITED OFFER".

## CTA button copy

Always: **"Read the full guide →"** or **"Reorder now →"** or **"Shop [category] →"**
Never: "Click here", "Buy now", "Order today".

## Signature/footer

Every email ends with the same footer. Never change it without explicit instruction.
