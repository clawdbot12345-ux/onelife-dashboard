# Ordered But Not Moving

**R142,746 tied up in 50 products** that keep getting reordered despite not selling. This is a PAR level problem — someone is reordering on autopilot.

## Worst Offenders

| Product | Orders This Year | Units on Shelf | Issue |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|-------|
| MAG 7/87 60'S | 10 | 86 | Ordered 10 times, sitting on shelf |
| 1L GUT RAWBIOTICS | 4 | 55 | |
| FLORISH SPORE PROBIOTIC 60C | 5 | 23 | |

## The Problem

These products are being repeatedly ordered despite having sufficient (or excess) stock. Every reorder ties up more capital in inventory that isn't generating revenue.

**R142,746 in dead capital** could be redirected to:
- Stocking high-margin products (see [[Operations/Knowledge/stock/double-down]])
- Filling actual stockout gaps (see [[Operations/Knowledge/stock/stockout-risks]])
- Marketing and growth initiatives

## Root Cause

- PAR levels set too high or never adjusted
- Automated reordering without sales velocity checks
- No feedback loop between sales data and purchasing decisions

## Recommended Actions

1. **Immediately stop reordering MAG 7/87 60'S** — 86 units with 10 orders this year is egregious
2. **Freeze reorders** on all 50 items until current stock sells through
3. **Review PAR levels** — whoever controls reorder points needs to see this data
4. **Set up a monthly "dead stock" review** as part of the procurement cycle
