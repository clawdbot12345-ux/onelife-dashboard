# Margin Warnings

Products with gross margin below acceptable thresholds. Average gross margin across the business is **35.5%**.

## Threshold Definitions

| Level | Margin | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Healthy | ≥40% | Push — see [[Operations/Knowledge/stock/double-down]] |
| Acceptable | 20-39% | Monitor |
| Warning | <20% | Review pricing or negotiate cost |
| Critical | <0% (negative) | Selling at a loss — immediate action |

## Key Insights

- Products below 20% margin need a pricing review — either the sell price is too low or the cost is too high
- Negative margin products are being sold at a loss. This might be intentional (loss leader) or an error.
- Check whether low-margin products are high-velocity sellers — sometimes low margin x high volume is acceptable
- Cross-reference with [[Operations/Knowledge/stock/ordered-not-moving]] — don't keep reordering low-margin dead stock

## Recommended Actions

1. **Review cost prices** with suppliers — negotiate better rates on warning/critical items
2. **Adjust sell prices** where the market allows — check competitor pricing
3. **Discontinue** negative-margin items unless they serve a clear strategic purpose (traffic driver, bundling anchor)
4. **Redirect shelf space** from low-margin to high-margin products ([[Operations/Knowledge/stock/double-down]])
