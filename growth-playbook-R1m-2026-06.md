## 4. Research findings (best-in-class, local + international)

### 4.1 Conversion & checkout levers (sourced, figures labeled)
- **Baymard (50-study meta-average):** 70.2% cart abandonment is normal; among
  checkout abandoners the top causes are surprise costs (39%), slow delivery
  (21%), payment trust (19%), forced account creation (19%) — all addressable.
  Large stores can recover ~35% more conversions through checkout design alone.
- **Benchmarks (Littledata):** all-Shopify median ~1.4%; health/supplements
  median 1.8–2.4%; top quartile 5%+. One Life at 0.48% = below all-Shopify
  median → basic checkout/trust hygiene is the priority tier, exactly as the
  funnel data showed.
- **SA free-shipping norms:** Faithful to Nature R400 · Wellness Warehouse R400
  · Clicks R350 (collect)/R650 (courier) · Dis-Chem ~R600. One Life's R400 is
  correctly positioned; NRF data: 51% of shoppers ADD items to reach a
  threshold → market the threshold harder in cart ("R86 away from free delivery").
- **BNPL SA:** Payflex pricing ~4.85% + R4/txn; vendor-claims +30% AOV (RBC
  independent estimate: BNPL lifts conversion 20–30%). PayJustNow fees
  undisclosed — negotiate. Happy Pay (pay-in-2, ad-subsidised) raised $5M
  in Mar 2026 — worth a meeting. SA BNPL market growing ~13.6%/yr.
- **Reviews (Spiegel/Northwestern study):** product with 5 reviews = +270%
  purchase likelihood vs zero; +380% for higher-priced items; benefit
  concentrates in the FIRST 5 reviews → seed hero SKUs first, exactly the
  review-blitz design.
- **Supplement trust badges:** 77% of consumers prefer third-party-certified
  supplements; 60% pay more for tested products → surface SAHPRA compliance +
  brand certifications as PDP badges.
- **Speed (Deloitte×Google):** 0.1s mobile speed improvement ≈ +8.4% retail
  conversion (correlational) — validates the performance work shipped today.
- **Quiz funnels:** price-before-recommendation sequencing tanked one
  supplement funnel's conversion 30% — keep quiz → recommendation → price order.

### 4.2 Subscriptions (sourced)
- Health & wellness = highest-AOV subscription vertical (Recharge: $63 AOV,
  $332/yr per subscriber, 4.75 products each — vendor data).
- Churn norms: 5–8%/mo typical, <3% best-in-class; ~20–40% of churn is FAILED
  PAYMENTS → dunning + card-updater is the cheapest retention money in the game.
- Discount structure: 10–15%, escalating/delayed beats big first-order discounts
  ("3rd month free" > "20% off now" — front-loading trains people to leave).
- Skip/pause/swap in the cancel flow saves 9.6–25% of cancellers.
- **Care/of post-mortem (shut down 2024 despite Bayer's $225m):** quizzes
  acquire but don't retain; personalization raised COGS; retention came from
  habit-framing + pre-shipment flexibility (Ritual >R250m gross 2024) instead.
- SA gap confirmed: FTN and Wellness Warehouse run NO real subscribe-&-save —
  the niche is open. (SA-specific risk: debit-order failure rates → dunning matters more here.)
- **The SA payments blocker (check before any app install):** Shopify's
  Subscriptions API only auto-charges via compliant gateways (Shopify
  Payments — NOT available in SA — PayPal Express, Authorize.net, select
  Stripe/Adyen). **PayFast does not support it.** Workable SA paths:
  (a) Paystack (most-cited SA-compatible gateway — verify directly),
  (b) Peach Payments recurring/tokenized (vendor-claimed),
  (c) zero-risk validation first: "reminder autoship" — Klaviyo flow at the
  customer's refill interval + one-click reorder link + locked 10% code.
  Costs nothing, works with the current gateway, proves demand.
- App economics at One Life's scale: Appstle or Seal (free tier, 0% txn
  fees) over Recharge ($99/mo + 1.49%/txn) until subscription MRR >R150k.
- Realistic mature target: 25–40% of online revenue on subscription within
  18–24 months (Ritual-like, not AG1-like). ~150–250 subscribers at R600
  AOV ≈ R90–150k/mo locked-in recurring.

### 4.3 WhatsApp commerce SA (sourced, de-hyped)
- Reach: 93–96% of SA internet users. Marketing template ~R0.68/message.
- **The "98% open rate" is a myth** (conflates delivery with reads). Real:
  60–68% reads, 15–25% CTR — still 3× email opens and 5–10× email clicks.
- Best documented SA case: Builders Warehouse post-purchase WhatsApp journey —
  70% engagement, 27% service-attach (vendor-claimed, brand-endorsed).
- Dis-Chem runs WhatsApp service since 2022 (no published numbers) — the
  health-retail precedent exists; nobody has nailed SELLING through it.
- Payment is the weak link: 90% of SA WhatsApp sellers settle via EFT/invoice;
  bridge = Ozow/Payfast payment links in-chat.
- One Life play: opt-in post-purchase journeys + replenishment nudges +
  consult-to-cart with payment links — Precious is already the WhatsApp brand.

### 4.4 Alternative revenue lines (sourced) — the outside-the-box section
1. **Retail media-lite** — sell placement to supplement brands: sponsored slots
   in emails/site + paid end-caps in stores. Precedent at scale: Shoprite
   Rainmaker R647m (+36.8% — audited); Dis-Chem Media exists in this exact
   channel. 70–90% margins. A 3-store chain version = brand packages
   (R10–30k/mo per brand: homepage slot + email feature + end-cap).
2. **Practitioner channel** — Fullscript crossed $1B revenue on practitioner
   dispensing; Thorne did ~53% of sales through professionals (SEC-audited).
   One Life's practitioner page exists; mechanic = referral codes with a
   15–25% practitioner split. Massively underexploited in SA.
3. **Vivid wholesale** — private label runs 25–30pts higher margin; SA contract
   manufacturers (MJ Labs, SA-Labs — SA-Labs white-label MOQ from just 50
   units) at MOQs from 50–1,000 units; second step = wholesale Vivid to
   independent pharmacies/gyms at 40–50% GM. The audited SA proof: **Clicks
   private label = 25.4% of total sales, 30.3% of front-shop** (FY24 annual
   report) — own-brand at scale is THE SA health-retail margin engine.
4. **Paid membership "One Life+"** — TakealotMORE anchors SA at R39–99/mo;
   NO paid tier exists in SA health retail (Dis-Chem's is free). McKinsey:
   paid-loyalty members 60% more likely to increase spend. Construct: R79–99/mo
   = member pricing + free delivery + quarterly consult credit.
5. **In-store diagnostics** — H&B × Randox model (10,000+ tests in 14 stores,
   drives supplement attach). SA analogue: partner Lancet/Ampath screening days.
   Sharper SA hook: **Discovery Vitality Health Check accreditation** —
   Vitality members earn points for BP/glucose/cholesterol checks at
   accredited independent pharmacies; accreditation pulls Discovery's
   member base into the 3 stores (terms unpublished — contact Discovery).
6. **Corporate wellness** — SA market $317–464m; pursue opportunistically
   (employer gifting, wellness-day pop-ups), not as a division.
AVOID (evidence-based): full marketplace (Boots shelved theirs), capital-heavy
IV/wellness studios (Restore closed 18 sites in 2024).

### 4.5 SA market & loyalty economics (sourced) — what the giants prove

**The market tailwind is real:**
- SA online retail: R96bn 2024 (+35%) → projected R130bn+ in 2025 (~10% of
  total retail), vs physical retail growing only 1.6–2.5%/yr (World Wide Worx
  / Mastercard — research-report, not audited). Online is where SA retail
  growth lives; the stores fund the play, online is the growth asset.
- SA supplements market ~R19–20bn, ~10% CAGR; online the fastest channel
  (~11% CAGR, vendor-est.). Dis-Chem's online is still only ~5% of revenue
  (analyst-reported) — the giants have NOT locked up health e-commerce.
- 46% of SA shoppers trust local platforms vs 10% overseas (survey) — a
  credible local brand beats iHerb on trust, not just shipping.

**Loyalty is THE proven SA health-retail mechanic:**
- **Clicks ClubCard: 12.9m active members driving 82%+ of all sales**
  (audited results). R438m cashback paid in just 6 months.
- **Dis-Chem Better Rewards** (relaunched Oct 2025, ~9m members): first 17
  weeks = R410m returned, 550k new/lapsed shoppers (Capitec partnership),
  participating-brand revenue +12%, volume +18.7% (company-reported).
  Dis-Chem front-loaded R330m into loyalty + "Health Hub" stores.
- Both giants run loyalty at 80%+ sales penetration. One Life's Smile
  program unified across 3 stores + online (Engine 4) is the scaled-down
  version of the single most-validated mechanic in SA health retail —
  and a paid tier (One Life+) is white space neither occupies.

**SA checkout reality (why Engine 1 is #1):**
- SA cart abandonment 83–84% vs ~75% global. **#1 SA-specific cause:
  payment failure — declined transactions implicated in ~52% of lost
  online sales** (Mastercard data via Stitch). Fixes that work: instant
  EFT (Ozow/Capitec Pay approve better than cards), payment-retry with a
  different method, guest checkout, delivery cost shown early.
- Payment mix is shifting: debit cards 29%, credit 19%, instant-EFT/A2A
  22% and growing fastest — card-only checkouts ignore half the market.
- Pargo: 4,000+ pickup points, ~20% cheaper than door delivery
  (vendor-claimed); click-and-collect fits One Life's 3-store footprint.
- Checkers Sixty60 (+47% to ~R19bn) reset Gauteng delivery expectations to
  same-day; store-fulfilled same-day within 10–15km of the 3 stores is the
  competitive answer courier-only rivals can't match.

**Marketplace channel (for Vivid only):**
- Takealot Health category success fee ~15% + R400/mo + fulfilment ≈
  20–25% all-in take — brutal for resold brands, viable for own-brand.
- **Amazon.co.za is running a promotional flat 5% referral fee** (one
  seller doc says to 31 Dec 2026, another to Mar 2027 — verify in Seller
  Central) and opened supplements in June 2025; Prime launched June 2026.
  A 5% window vs Takealot's ~15% = the cheapest marketplace acquisition
  channel SA has ever offered a supplement brand. Window closes — move early.
- Rule: list 10–20 high-margin Vivid/exclusive SKUs as a billboard; keep
  subscriptions + customer data on Shopify. Never list price-comparable
  third-party brands (the Bodybuilding.com death spiral, §4.6).

### 4.6 International winners — what actually worked (sourced)

**Holland & Barrett (the most transferable case):** £725m (FY22, loss-making)
→ £981m FY25, third straight year of double-digit growth; digital >21% of
revenue growing +20%/yr (2× the store base); AOV +25% after web/app rebuild.
What drove it: (1) own-brand pushed toward ~50% penetration — the margin
engine; (2) loyalty relaunched as instant-redemption + member pricing
(vouchers abolished, points spendable immediately); (3) stores became health
hubs — Randox in-store diagnostics (10k+ tests in 14 stores), £39–79 blood
tests with FREE in-store consultant follow-up (the store-to-online repeat
loop). Honest caveat: EBITDA was flat across the whole run — growth was
bought with the owner's £700m debt wipe-out + ~£290m capex. Copy the
mechanics, not the spending.

**iHerb:** $2.9bn FY25 (+19%), 73% of orders from REPEAT customers, 7.9%
conversion, and **subscriptions up 370% YoY — its single biggest growth
lever** (company-claimed). Autoship discount: just 5% (promos to 10%) —
you don't need to give away margin to make replenishment work.

**Ritual / AG1:** subscription-default checkout, ~92% repeat claims,
ingredient-traceability as the trust signal. **Care/of (shut by Bayer
2024)** proves quizzes acquire but don't retain — habit-framing +
pre-shipment flexibility retains.

**Huel's warning:** its DTC site plateaued (~0–5% growth) while wholesale
doubled — even great DTC brands hit a site ceiling; One Life's analogue
expansion paths are practitioner wholesale + Vivid into independent
pharmacies (§4.4).

**The Vitamin Shoppe:** >90% of sales from loyalty members, 10% autoship
discount, in-store subscription enrollment AT THE TILL since 2017 — the
exact mechanic for Engine 4's counter capture. Yet the chain sold for just
$193.5m in its parent's bankruptcy — loyalty alone doesn't save you if the
products are price-comparable everywhere (own-brand is the moat).

**GNC ship-from-store:** 95% of online orders now ship from stores (was
26%); transit 4 days → 1.5; shipping costs −10% (company-claimed). With
three Gauteng stores holding live stock, One Life can match this on day
one — same-day Gauteng delivery from store shelves.

**Cautionary tales (what kills health retailers online):**
- **Bodybuilding.com:** $226m → ~$40m. Cause: selling identical third-party
  SKUs that Amazon price-matched, no subscription/loyalty/own-brand moat.
- **Vitamin Shoppe parent:** Chapter 11. Same lesson.
- The pattern: every winner has own-brand + subscription + loyalty; every
  loser sold other people's products on price. Vivid + protocols +
  consultant trust IS One Life's version of the moat.
