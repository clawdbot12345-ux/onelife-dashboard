# Vivid Health — South Africa Competitive Audit (June 2026)

> A field map of who Vivid is fighting, what they charge, where they're weak, and where the SA market still has wide-open lanes. Prices are best-found public listings (VAT inclusive), normalised to dose-per-Rand where possible.

---

## 1. Faithful to Nature — `faithful-to-nature.co.za`

- **Positioning:** SA's biggest "ethical, organic, eco" marketplace — Africa's first B Corp retailer.
- **Price points (representative SKUs they stock):**
  - Vitamin C 1000mg: R125-R250 (60-100 tabs)
  - Magnesium glycinate: R220-R450 (60 caps)
  - Omega-3: R220-R600
  - Multivitamin: R200-R750
  - Ashwagandha: R200-R380
  - Turmeric: R180-R350
- **UX strengths:** Strong taxonomy ("by ingredient", "by concern", "by lifestyle: vegan/keto/banting"); editorial-style PDPs; ingredient transparency badges; "Verified by FtN" filter.
- **UX weaknesses:** Marketplace creates SKU bloat — 12,000+ products dilute curation; bot/anti-scrape returns 403 to many crawlers, hurting Google Shopping and AI surfacing; no dose-slider filter; checkout still triggers Hellopeter loyalty complaints.
- **Content/SEO:** Heavy blog (200+ posts), strong ranking on "natural", "vegan", "eco" long-tails; weaker on clinical/condition queries vs. Healthline-style content.
- **Trust:** B Corp badge, Hellopeter 7.7, ingredient sourcing notes; no SAHPRA badges (they're a retailer, not registrant).
- **Subscription/loyalty:** Faithful Friends points programme; **no auto-replenish subscription**.
- **Social:** IG `@faithfultonature` ~118K; consistent reels but lifestyle/eco content, not supplement-led; TikTok minimal.
- **Gaps:** No subscribe-&-save, no quiz-to-stack, no clinical evidence layer, no dose-per-Rand comparison.

## 2. Wellness Warehouse — `wellnesswarehouse.com`

- **Positioning:** "Live Life Well" — premium clean-living retailer with 40+ physical stores.
- **Price points:**
  - Vitamin C 1000mg (Solgar/Solal): R180-R380
  - Magnesium (NOW Inositol Relax 454g): **R829.95**; magnesium glycinate 60s ~R250-R450
  - Omega-3 (Solgar): R350-R650
  - Multivitamin (Solgar/WW house): R250-R900
  - Ashwagandha: R220-R420
  - Turmeric: R200-R400
- **UX strengths:** Best-in-SA filtering by concern + lifestyle + form; in-house "WW Range" private label; "Eligible for discount" collection; tier-based loyalty UX.
- **UX weaknesses:** Cluttered nav (3 levels of mega-menu); migrating loyalty system has caused downtime; PDPs heavy on marketing copy, light on independent clinical refs.
- **Content/SEO:** "Wellness Hub" blog; ranks well on "clean supplements", "wellness gifts"; weaker on condition pages vs. Healthline-style competitors.
- **Trust:** Pharmacist-staffed stores, in-store nutritionists; SAHPRA references on registered SKUs; reviews on product pages.
- **Subscription/loyalty:** **Live Life Well Rewards** tiered cashback, double on house range. No auto-ship.
- **Social:** IG `@wellnesswarehouse` ~51K; TikTok `@wellness_warehousesa` ~1.8K (under-invested); cadence 4-5x/week.
- **Gaps:** No subscription, no quiz, weak TikTok, no "skip the queue" auto-refill — the obvious play for a 40-store chain.

## 3. Dis-Chem — `dischem.co.za` (supplements)

- **Positioning:** Mass-market pharmacy giant; supplements via house brands (Biogen, Bettaway) + 3rd party.
- **Price points:**
  - Biogen Vit C 1000mg 60 caps: ~R110-R150
  - Bettaway Mega C 1000mg 60s: **R169.95**
  - Solal Magnesium Glycinate 60s: **R449.99**
  - Solal Ashwagandha 60 caps: R350-R450
  - Biogen Omega-3: R150-R400
  - Multivitamins: R120 (Bettaway) to R600+ (Solal/Solgar)
- **UX strengths:** Massive catalogue, ClubCard integration, "Benefit" loyalty (10% on 140+ brands across 10,000 SKUs from Oct 2025 relaunch), Discovery/Vitality stacking.
- **UX weaknesses:** Dated, cluttered grid; no dose filter; mediocre on-site search; PDPs templated and bland; no clinical context.
- **Content/SEO:** Thin — relies on retail brand authority; ranks for transactional ["biogen vit c 1000mg"] but not condition queries.
- **Trust:** Pharmacist association (SAPC), SAHPRA where applicable, but commerce-first not science-first; reviews enabled but sparse.
- **Subscription/loyalty:** **Better Rewards** (Oct 2025) — instant till discounts, medical-aid linked savings up to R3,000/month. No auto-ship.
- **Social:** IG ~500K+ (mass retailer); content is promo-heavy, not category-led.
- **Gaps:** No premium curation, no subscription, no editorial, no quiz — they win on price/distribution, lose on brand love.

## 4. Clicks — `clicks.co.za` (supplements)

- **Positioning:** Mass-market chemist; "feel good, pay less."
- **Price points:**
  - Clicks Vit C 1000mg 100 tabs: ~R95-R120 (cheapest mainstream)
  - Clicks Expert Magnesium Glycinate 60: **R199**
  - Vital Magnesium Complex 90s: ~R150
  - Vital Maxi C 1000mg 30s: **R156.90**
  - Solal Omega-All 60: R439
  - Multivitamins: R80 (Clicks house) to R500+
- **UX strengths:** Clean grid, ClubCard frictionless, fastest delivery in mass, app activations for personalised deals.
- **UX weaknesses:** Supplements buried under "Vitamins & Supplements" with no concern-led discovery; product imagery inconsistent; PDPs minimal; weak storytelling.
- **Content/SEO:** "Health Hub" condition pages exist and rank well on "vitamin C benefits"-type queries — best of the mass retailers.
- **Trust:** SA's **most-used loyalty programme** for 27 years (ClubCard); pharmacist-backed; SAHPRA references on registered.
- **Subscription/loyalty:** ClubCard (R5 = 1 point, double Wednesday cashback); no auto-ship.
- **Social:** IG ~700K+; promo-led, not supplement-specific.
- **Gaps:** Same as Dis-Chem — distribution wins, brand and curation lose.

## 5. Solal — `solal.co.za`

- **Positioning:** Premium, doctor-formulated nutraceutical brand (Dr Brent Murphy's legacy).
- **Price points:**
  - Magnesium Glycinate 60: **R349-R449**
  - Omega-3 Krill 60: **R379-R439**
  - Vit D3 1000IU 60: R130-R147
  - "3 Per Day" Multivit 90: R400-R600
  - Double Vit C 90: **R339.95**
  - Ashwagandha 60: ~R350
- **UX strengths:** Doctor-led storytelling; conditions-mapped product navigation; clinical references on PDPs.
- **UX weaknesses:** Site is brochureware — pushes you to Dis-Chem/Wellness Warehouse for purchase, losing margin and data; dated design; no subscription.
- **Content/SEO:** Strong clinical content backbone; ranks for ingredient queries.
- **Trust:** SAHPRA-registered SKUs, doctor-formulated narrative, third-party retailer reviews.
- **Subscription/loyalty:** None direct (retailer-dependent).
- **Social:** IG `@solal_sa` ~8K — under-leveraged for the brand equity.
- **Gaps:** No DTC, no subscription, weak social, no community — a sleeping giant.

- **Positioning:** "SA's premium boutique apothecary" — 10,000+ SKUs, 250+ brands, 30yrs family, free consults.

- **UX strengths:** Condition collections (`/collections/conditions-...`), free consultant CTAs, blog `health-wellness-hub` (ranks for "best magnesium SA 2026").
- **UX weaknesses:** Marketplace breadth dilutes own brand; PDPs vary by brand quality; no quiz, no subscription, no proprietary stack.

- **Social:** IG `@onelifehealthandnutrition` ~556 + `@one_life_health_supermarket` ~1.4K — **dangerously under-invested**.
- **Gaps:** Owns retail, doesn't own brand. Vivid's job is to invert this.

## SA Market Table-Stakes (Vivid Must Match)

| Capability | Why |
|---|---|
| Free delivery >R400 | Set by FtN/Onelife/WW; below this customers won't checkout |
| ClubCard/Vitality stacking story | Discovery/Vitality membership = 70%+ of premium spend |
| SAHPRA-registered SKUs on regulated categories | Trust floor |
| Pharmacist or nutritionist consult (chat/email) | Onelife/WW/Dis-Chem all offer it |
| Hellopeter presence ≥7.5 | Where SA buyers verify before purchase |
| EFT + Payfast/Yoco/Mobicred/PayJustNow | "Pay in 3" now table-stakes |
| Same-day Cape Town/JHB delivery | Set by WW/FtN |
| Condition-led collections (sleep / energy / immunity / gut / women's) | Universal navigation pattern |
| Local toll-free + WhatsApp support | Cultural expectation |

## SA Market Gaps (Vivid's Wedge)

1. **Subscription / auto-replenish is virtually absent** — no major SA player runs true subscribe-&-save with discount (vs. Ritual, AG1, Care/of, Athletic Greens globally). Massive open lane.
2. **No quiz-to-stack experience** — Care/of, Persona, Rootine are standard internationally. Zero SA equivalents.
3. **Dose-per-Rand transparency is non-existent** — no SA brand normalises mg/serving cost. Easy differentiator.
4. **TikTok is wide open** — even WW's `@wellness_warehousesa` is at ~1.8K. The leader in supplement TikTok-SA could own the under-35 segment uncontested.
5. **Founder-led / scientific advisor content** is sparse — Solal had it, has not modernised. No Huberman-style longform from a SA brand.
6. **Third-party lab COA badges per batch** — international gold standard (Thorne, Momentous), absent in SA.
7. **Stack bundles + bundle-builder UI** — globally normal, no SA player does it.
8. **Refill-pouch / lower-carbon SKU** — eco-positioning lane open beyond FtN's commodity stance.
9. **Clinical evidence layer on PDPs** (PubMed cited, dose-response shown) — only Solal/Coyne attempt; both are unmodernised.
10. **Genuine community / closed Discord/WhatsApp** — zero SA brands run this.

## Price Band Map — Where Vivid Should Slot

| Tier | Price band (Vit C 1000mg, 60 ct) | Players | Vivid play |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cheap** | R80-R150 | Clicks house, Bettaway, Cipla | Don't compete. No margin, no story. |
| **Mid** | R150-R280 | Biogen, Vital, NutriZen, The Real Thing | Vivid **flagship tier** — match here on hero SKUs (~R199-R249), beat on UX, design, evidence, subscription discount. |
| **Premium** | R280-R450 | Solal, Coyne, Solgar, Terranova | Vivid **"Vivid Pro" tier** for chelates, methylated B12, magnesium L-threonate, NMN. Compete on novel/branded actives. |
| **Ultra-premium** | R450+ | Solal complexes, imported (Thorne, Pure Encapsulations grey imports) | Optional 2027 lane via practitioner-grade. |

**Sweet spot for Vivid hero stacks**: bundle 3 SKUs at R599-R799 with 15% subscribe-&-save → AOV R650+ and LTV through auto-ship.

## Top 5 SA Brands to Position Directly Against

1. **Onelife Health** — sister brand, same retail breadth. Vivid is the **branded modern apothecary** to Onelife's marketplace. Don't cannibalise; differentiate on quiz + subscription + design.
2. **Solal** — best clinical legacy in SA, weakest modern execution. Steal their evidence-led credibility with a 2026 UX.
3. **Wellness Warehouse (WW Range house brand)** — closest premium retail competitor; beat them on subscription + TikTok.
4. **The Real Thing** — owns "natural/heritage" — Vivid takes "natural + modern + measurable".
5. **Coyne / Phytoceutics** — owns practitioner credibility — Vivid takes practitioner credibility *plus* consumer love.

---

**Strategic summary for Vivid:** The SA supplement category is structurally weak on six dimensions every serious international DTC nails — subscription, quiz/personalisation, dose transparency, TikTok, batch COAs, and bundle UX. Match the table-stakes in 90 days; ship two of the gaps in 180; own four within 12 months and Vivid is the category-defining SA brand.
