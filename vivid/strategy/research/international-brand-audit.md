# Best-in-Class Health/Supplement Brand Audit — for Vivid Health (ZA)

Sourced from each brand's public pages plus reputable industry analysis (Nutraingredients, Latterly, BeautyMatter, This Is Landscape, Daily Coffee News, TechCrunch, PRNewswire).

---

## 1. AG1 / Athletic Greens (drinkag1.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Minimalist black-on-white with brand green accent; hero is a single hyper-styled product still — the canister, scoop and shaker on a flat ground. Premium "tech product" treatment more akin to Apple than supplements. Sans-serif throughout, generous whitespace. No clutter, no lifestyle models above the fold.
- **IA**: Effectively single-SKU. Nav is shallow: Shop / Why AG1 / Science / Subscribe. Collection logic is irrelevant because there is one hero product (and a few bundles/extensions).
- **Differentiation hooks**: "75 ingredients in one scoop" (Next Gen formula spring 2026 = 83 ingredients) — leads with comprehensiveness. NSF Certified for Sport, podcast endorsements (Rogan, Huberman) used as third-party trust.
- **Conversion mechanics**: Subscription-first ($79/month for subscribers vs $99 one-time). **Welcome Kit** — canister, scoop, leak-resistant shaker — is the seductive onboarding hook. 90-day money-back: *"Taste it. Feel the difference. Decide."* Price-anchored to "<$3/day".
- **Email/social**: Heavy podcast-host social proof; founder Chris Ashenden rarely fronts.
- **Steal**: Single-SKU clarity + welcome kit as physical onboarding ritual + price-per-day reframing.

## 2. Ritual (ritual.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Signature dusty pastel palette (warm yellow, blush, off-white). Distinctive **animated capsule** — oil/beadlets visibly swirling — is the brand's most-stolen motion. Editorial typography (custom serif + clean grotesque). Studio product photography only; few models.
- **IA**: Nav is by **life stage** (Daily Health, Pregnancy by trimester, Postpartum) + secondary by need (gut, skin, relaxation). "Bundle & Save" is its own destination.
- **Differentiation hooks**: **"Made Traceable®"** — publishes supplier name and final manufacturing location for every ingredient. "2.6 Billion Capsules Sold." University-led clinical studies cited. Clean Label Project, B-Corp, vegan/non-GMO badges.
- **SEO/content**: Editorial blog/journal "The Journal" on ingredient science and women's health.
- **Conversion**: Subscribe-first (full retail otherwise). Up to 30% off bundle subscriptions. Discount applies to first order only — predictable LTV play.
- **Steal**: Traceable supply chain page (single most copyable trust play); life-stage IA; the swirling-capsule animation as a hero motif.

## 3. Seed (seed.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Editorial science magazine meets Apple keynote. Cream/off-white background, sparse type, micro-to-macro photography ("juxtaposition of internal and external worlds" per their design partner Landscape). Capsule cross-section illustration is signature.
- **IA**: Quiz CTA + direct shop. SKUs use clinical naming (DS-01®, DM-02™, AM-02™, PM-02™) — pharma-grade taxonomy.
- **PDP (DS-01)**: 38.7 billion AFU across 4 functional blends (Digestive, Dermatological, Cardiovascular, Micronutrient). **ViaCap®** capsule-in-capsule explained with cutaway. 4.8 stars, 13,323 reviews. $49.99/30-day.
- **Differentiation hooks**: Scientific Advisory Board front-and-center (Dr. Gregor Reid — ex-WHO probiotic panel chair; Dr. Jacques Ravel; Dr. James Versalovic). FDA IND for IBS trial. **Refillable glass jar + travel vial** in welcome kit, monthly compostable refill pouch.
- **Conversion**: Subscription default. 25% bundle discount.
- **Steal**: Refill model + scientific advisory board page + clinical SKU naming (signals seriousness without saying "trust us").

## 4. Thorne (thorne.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Clinical, restrained. White/navy palette, sport-performance hero rotates with women's health. Athletic studio photography.
- **IA**: Dual-track (Sports Performance prominent; women's health secondary). **Quick Order** in nav signals power users / practitioners. **Taia AI advisor** sits in nav as conversational entry.
- **Differentiation hooks**: NSF Certified for Sport on 20+ SKUs — the most in the category. "Four rounds of testing." TGA (Australia) and GRMA references. **At-home health tests** (gut, biological age) are a unique upper-funnel offer.
- **Conversion**: Subscribe & Save up to 20% + free shipping. FSA/HSA payment via Truemed — US-specific but worth noting as a B2B2C insurance angle.
- **Email/social**: Educational content hub "Take 5 Daily."
- **Steal**: AI advisor in nav; at-home test → personalized supplement loop; NSF Certified for Sport as a visible badge category.

## 5. Pure Encapsulations (pureencapsulations.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Deliberately clinical — white background, blue accent, pharma-grade. No lifestyle photography on landing; bottles only.
- **IA**: Site **forks at the door** — Healthcare Professional vs Consumer (separate sub-sites). Strongest practitioner positioning in category.
- **Differentiation hooks**: **"FREE FROM unnecessary additives and many common allergens"** = the "hypoallergenic" claim, owned for 34 years. Atrium Innovations / Nestlé Health Science backing. Available via Fullscript practitioner dispensary platform.
- **Conversion**: Practitioner dispensing rather than DTC subscription as primary path.
- **Steal**: The forked entry (practitioner vs consumer) — relevant for Vivid if pharmacists/dietitians are a sell-in channel. Pharma-grade visual restraint as differentiation when competitors all go pastel.

## 6. MaryRuth Organics (maryruthorganics.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Warm naturals (peach, sage, cream) with hand-drawn fruit illustrations. Lifestyle photography of multi-generational family use. Friendly serifs + script accents.
- **IA**: Triple-axis collection logic — **(a) life stage** (infant → teens → prenatal → 40+), **(b) format** (liquid, gummy, capsule, liposomal), **(c) focus** (immunity, calm, beauty, gut). Most exhaustive segmentation in category.
- **Differentiation hooks**: Liquid multivitamin = the hero format (32oz bottles, $42.95–$74.95). USDA Organic, Clean Label Project, B-Corp, woman-owned, family-founded narrative (MaryRuth Ghiyam, mom of 4, health educator).
- **Conversion**: Free shipping $49+ (US). Subscribe & Save 10% auto-discount. Wellness Rewards loyalty program. Bundle discounts 5–20%.
- **SEO/content**: "The Wellness Drop" blog (recipes + nutrition).
- **Steal**: Life-stage × format × focus matrix navigation; liquid as hero format for hard-to-swallow segments; loyalty points.

## 7. Olly (olly.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: The brightest brand in the audit. Color-per-goal system (yellow Mood, blue Sleep, pink Beauty) pulled straight from physical packaging — the website palette literally inherits from the bottles. Smiley face inside the "O." Friendly bubble sans-serif. Surreal still-life photography (Dan Saelinger collaborations).
- **IA**: Nav is **strictly by goal** — Sleep, Mood, Women's Health, Gut Health, Energy, Cognitive, Beauty, Kids. Format (gummies) is implicit.
- **Copy tone**: Playful, never clinical. SKU names: "Hello Happy," "Mellow Menopause," "Nighty Night." Disclaimer asterisk acknowledged but de-emphasized.
- **Conversion**: Free shipping $59+. "Buy 3 Get 1 Free" (code BUNDLE26). Subscribe & Save. Retail-style pricing $9.99–$19.99 (drugstore anchor).
- **Steal**: Goal-color system that bridges shelf and screen; mass-market-friendly SKU naming; tiered bundle promos.

## 8. HUM Nutrition (humnutrition.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Clean neutral base with bright accent moments (peach pink "Big Peach Energy" hero). Modern sans-serif, soft product still life.
- **IA**: Strict **by-concern** taxonomy (Skin → acne/dryness/firmness/fine lines; Body/Gut → debloat/digestion; Mood → sleep/stress/energy; Women's Health; Hair + Nails). Specific symptoms not categories.
- **Differentiation hooks**: **3-minute quiz** + **free unlimited RD nutritionist access** post-quiz (Gaby Vaca-Flores RD as named expert). "Verified Clean, Clinically Tested, Triple Tested & 3rd Party Validated." Clean Label, Non-GMO, Prevented Ocean Plastic packaging.
- **Conversion**: Subscribe & Save 30% on every order via bundle. Reviews show count + stars (4.4–4.7, "2.5K reviews" on hero SKUs).
- **Steal**: Free human nutritionist as post-purchase retention magnet; symptom-level (not condition-level) IA.

## 9. Care/of (takecareof.com) — CAUTIONARY CASE

- **Status**: Bayer shut Care/of down 17 June 2024 after acquiring 70% for $225M in 2020. Site is dark.
- **What worked before**: 6-minute quiz across diet/sleep/stress/fitness/concerns → personalized daily packets with the customer's **first name printed on each tear-open packet**. ~50 ingredients in the catalog; typical pack was 4–7 supplements.
- **What failed**: Unit economics. Personalization at SKU scale ran heavy fulfillment costs; the category commoditized.
- **Steal cautiously**: Named packet personalization is powerful brand UX *but* don't build the business model on free-quiz → ultra-fragmented packs. Use named packets as a premium tier or for the highest-margin bundle.

## 11. Goop Wellness (shop.goop.com/wellness)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Editorial magazine. Warm cream + black serif/grotesque mix. Big editorial photography, product as still life. Founder (Gwyneth Paltrow) front and center as voice — first-person product descriptions ("I'm wiped out…").
- **IA**: Nav by lifestyle category (Beauty, Wellness, Style, Food, Travel) — supplements live inside Wellness.
- **Differentiation hooks**: Daily protocol packs at premium prices — **"The Mother Load" prenatal at $90/month**, packets bundle 6 different pills (Mother Multi, Cal-Mag, Choline, two Omega-3s). Founder voice = differentiation. (Note: has drawn regulatory scrutiny — ASA UK breaches — relevant warning for Vivid's claim copy.)
- **Steal**: Founder-as-narrator product copy; daily pre-portioned packets at premium price as the format itself; editorial PDP.

## 12. Wellbeing Nutrition (wellbeingnutrition.com) — closest market analog

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Bright green + blue palette with strong product photography. Hero is offer-driven ("48-Hour Flash Sale — FLAT 15% OFF").
- **IA**: Nav by **Benefit** (Sleep, Gut, Weight, Sports Performance, Beauty) + **Format category** (Melts, Effervescents, Proteins, Collagen, Vitamins, Kids).
- **Differentiation hooks**: **Format innovation as positioning** — "Melts" (oral thin strips that dissolve on tongue) and "Slow" (time-release capsules) are owned product categories. "Traceable ingredients, scientifically-backed, no sugar/preservatives/fillers, non-GMO, lab tested" repeated as 6-pillar promise. US FDA-registered facility (claim emphasized despite being India-based).
- **Conversion**: 3-tier pack sizing (2/4/6) with escalating discount (5/10/15%). **Free consultation**, Refer & Earn, mobile app.
- **Steal**: Format-as-category (Melts/Slow) — Vivid could own a format the same way. Tier-pack discount ladder is the cleanest version of bundle pricing. Free consultation as conversion path.

## 13. Persona Nutrition (personanutrition.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Sage green accent on neutral palette. Diverse customers + real nutritionists in approachable (not clinical) photography.
- **IA**: Two front-door CTAs — "Book a nutritionist" or "Take the 5-min quiz." Dual path is the headline mechanic.
- **Differentiation hooks**: **Screens against 4,000+ drug-nutrient interactions** before any pack ships. Team-led ("doctors, pharmacists, and nutritionists, refined over a decade"). 10,000+ Trustpilot reviews (4.4 stars). Owned by Nestlé Health Science (same parent as Pure Encapsulations).
- **Conversion**: Subscription default. "Skip, pause, cancel anytime." 20% off first or "Buy 2, Get 1 FREE."
- **Steal**: Drug-interaction screen as a safety claim (huge in pharmacy-adjacent ZA market). Dual quiz / human-consult path.

## 14. Bulletproof (bulletproof.com)

- **Visual / aesthetic**: Post-rebrand (June 2025, under Bia Foods) the palette is darker / warmer with accent colors. "Blended for Flavor. Brewed for Function." headline — the rebrand explicitly **abandoned the mold/toxin/biohacker hard sell** for "energizing, accessible wellness." Lifestyle imagery of coffee in mugs replacing lab/biohacking shots.
- **IA**: Coffee × Supplements × MCT × Mix-ins. Supplements organized by benefit (immunity, stress, sleep, brain, gut).
- **Differentiation hooks**: "58k 5-Star Reviews | 500M+ Cups." Toxin-tested coffee remains but de-emphasized. Omnichannel: "Coffee + Creatine now in Target nationwide."
- **Conversion**: Subscribe up to 30% off, tiered savings.
- **SEO/content**: Heavy blog — "Does Coffee Affect GLP-1?", time-of-day longevity guides — riding adjacent search trends (GLP-1, longevity, sleep).
- **Steal**: SEO content piggybacking adjacent trending topics (GLP-1, longevity); review-count-as-headline social proof.

## SYNTHESIS

### The 12 patterns that recur in 3+ brands (table-stakes Vivid must match)

1. **Subscribe-first pricing architecture** — subscribers get 10–30% off; one-off price acts as anchor (AG1, Ritual, Seed, MaryRuth, HUM, Persona, Bulletproof, Thorne).
2. **Free-shipping threshold tuned to AOV** — $49/$59/$89 depending on basket (MaryRuth, Olly, Wellbeing).
3. **Bundle ladder** — 2/4/6-pack or "Buy 3 Get 1" with escalating discount (Wellbeing, Olly, Persona).
4. **Quiz as primary acquisition path** — 3–6 minutes, outputs personalized recommendation + email capture (HUM, Persona, Seed, Care/of, Thorne via Taia).
5. **Multi-axis collection navigation** — by health goal AND by life stage AND/or format (Ritual, MaryRuth, Olly, HUM, Wellbeing).
6. **Trust badge stack on PDP** — third-party testing, Clean Label / non-GMO / vegan / B-Corp / NSF, displayed as a 4–6 icon row (Ritual, MaryRuth, Seed, HUM, Wellbeing, Thorne).
7. **Reviews with count in the thousands shown above the fold** — 4.4–4.8 stars, count formatted "13K," "2.5K," "58k" (Seed, HUM, Bulletproof, Persona).
8. **Money-back guarantee 30–90 days** as risk-reversal in hero (AG1 "Taste it. Feel the difference. Decide.", others).
9. **Capsule-or-jar product photography as hero**, no model — clinical / pharma styling beats lifestyle for trust (AG1, Ritual, Seed, Pure, Thorne).
10. **Founder/expert voice as proof** — named scientist, RD or founder pictured (Ritual founders, Seed advisors, HUM's Gaby Vaca-Flores RD, Goop's Gwyneth, MaryRuth herself, Asprey legacy).
11. **Welcome Kit / first-month ritual** — physical onboarding includes branded canister/jar/shaker, refill thereafter (AG1, Seed).
12. **Editorial blog as SEO engine** — ingredient deep-dives, condition guides, routine content (Bulletproof, Ritual, MaryRuth, Goop, HUM).

### The 6 differentiators only 1–2 brands do well (opportunities for Vivid)

1. **Made Traceable®-style supply chain page** (Ritual). Publishing supplier name + factory location per ingredient is one of the most defensible trust moats in the category. **No South African brand does this.**
2. **Single-SKU clarity at premium price** (AG1). Resisting the urge to launch 12 SKUs and instead nailing one all-in-one with daily-ritual positioning is rare and brutally effective.
3. **Format ownership as positioning** (Wellbeing's Melts/Slow; Seed's ViaCap; AG1's powder). Owning a delivery format — not an ingredient — is harder to copy than a formulation.
4. **Free human expert (RD) post-purchase** (HUM, Persona). Unlimited free nutritionist access converts trial to subscription far better than a chatbot. Persona's drug-interaction screen is a near-clinical safety claim.
5. **Refillable jar + travel vial economy** (Seed). Sustainability + branded ritual + lower COGS on month 2 onward. Works in ZA with rising eco-consciousness.
6. **Forked practitioner / consumer entry** (Pure Encapsulations). For Vivid, this could translate into a pharmacist/dietitian portal — a B2B2C wedge no DTC competitor in ZA owns.

### Practical recommendation for Vivid

Combine **Seed's editorial science aesthetic** with **Ritual's traceable supply chain trust play**, **Olly's goal-color IA** for mass approachability, **HUM's free-RD post-purchase mechanic** for retention, **Wellbeing's tier-pack discount ladder** for conversion, and a **single-SKU "Vivid Daily" hero** AG1-style anchoring the brand — surrounded by a small, disciplined range. Avoid Goop's claim risk and Care/of's quiz-overbuilt fulfillment trap.
