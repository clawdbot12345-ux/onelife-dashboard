Vivid vs the supermarket multivitamin

Most chemist and supermarket multivitamins use a wide-ingredient, low-dose model — many vitamins and minerals at small amounts in one capsule. The trade-off:

  • You get convenience (one bottle)
  • You lose dose-specificity (most actives are too low to do anything therapeutic)
  • You lose form-specificity (magnesium oxide instead of magnesium glycinate, etc.)
  • You cannot adjust upward when you need more of a specific nutrient

Vivids approach is: identify the one or two specific outcomes you are working on, take a single-focus formulation at the trial dose for 6–8 weeks, then reassess. You will spend more on each bottle, but you will use what you buy.

Vivid vs proprietary-blend supplements

A "proprietary blend" is when a label tells you the combined total weight of multiple actives — e.g. "Adrenal Support Blend 800mg" — without telling you the milligrams of each. This is legal but tells you nothing useful. The brand could be using 750mg of cheap rice bran and 50mg of the active that does the work.

Vivid never uses proprietary blends. If a Vivid label says it has Withania Somnifera, it tells you 300mg of Withania Somnifera root extract standardised to 5% withanolides. Every time.
