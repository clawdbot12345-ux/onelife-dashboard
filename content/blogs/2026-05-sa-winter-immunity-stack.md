# The South African Winter Immunity Stack — What Our Pharmacists Actually Recommend

**TL;DR:** Three supplements, each with a different job, each with proper evidence behind it. Vitamin C as your daily baseline, Zinc as your "first sign of symptoms" lever, and Elderberry as the 48-hour rescue. That's the stack our team actually recommends at the counter — no fluff, no 12-product pyramid schemes.

---

## Why SA winter hits differently

From April to September, the Highveld does a quiet number on your immunity:

- **Lower sun exposure** = lower natural vitamin D production (SA actually has surprisingly high vitamin D deficiency rates)
- **Dry indoor air** from heaters = dehydrated mucous membranes, which are your first line of defence against viruses
- **Temperature swings** of 15-20°C between daytime and early evening, which stresses the body's thermoregulation
- **More time indoors in closer proximity** = higher viral exposure
- **Fewer fresh fruits and vegetables** in a typical winter diet compared to summer

None of this is catastrophic on its own. But it compounds over 5-6 months, and the result is the predictable April-July bump in colds, flu, and low-grade fatigue that fills our Centurion store every morning.

## The stack — three products, three jobs

### 1. Vitamin C — the daily baseline
**Dose:** 500-1000 mg daily, with food, ideally split into morning and evening.

Vitamin C is the most-studied immune nutrient in the world. It's water-soluble, which means two things: you can't overdose on it (excess gets peed out), and you also can't "stock up" — you need a steady daily supply.

The evidence for vitamin C *preventing* colds in the general population is modest. The evidence for it *shortening* colds once you have one is much stronger — especially at higher doses taken regularly before symptom onset.

**Don't waste your money on:** 2000 mg mega-doses. Anything above ~500 mg at a single time just gets excreted. Split the dose.

### 2. Zinc — the first-sign lever
**Baseline dose:** 15-25 mg daily as a preventative during winter. **At first sign of symptoms:** 50-75 mg per day for 3-5 days.

Zinc is where most people under-dose and under-use. The research on zinc for colds is actually very solid — multiple meta-analyses show zinc lozenges or capsules taken within 24 hours of symptoms can reduce cold duration by 33%.

The form matters. Zinc picolinate, citrate and gluconate are all well-absorbed. Zinc oxide (common in cheap multivitamins) is poorly absorbed. And zinc should NOT be taken with calcium, iron or coffee — it competes for absorption with all three.

**Don't take it on an empty stomach if you're prone to nausea.** Split the dose if 50 mg upsets you.

### 3. Elderberry — the 48-hour rescue
**Dose:** Follow the product label. Typically 500-1000 mg of elderberry extract, 3-4 times per day, for 5-7 days at the first sign of a cold.

Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) has two randomised controlled trials showing it cuts the duration and severity of influenza symptoms when started within 48 hours. The mechanism is a direct antiviral effect plus immune modulation — it's one of the few herbal immune remedies with genuinely good clinical data.

**Important:** Elderberry is a *rescue* tool, not a daily supplement. Taking it chronically isn't necessary and may not be ideal. Save it for when you feel the first scratchy throat or fatigue hitting.

## What we don't recommend

- **Echinacea** — mixed evidence, inconsistent results. Not bad, but not where we'd spend your money first.
- **Colloidal silver** — no good human data, real safety concerns at high doses.
- **Mega-dose multivitamins** — you're better off with targeted single ingredients at clinical doses than a kitchen-sink multi at sub-clinical doses of everything.

## The stack in practice

**Daily through winter:** 500 mg Vitamin C + 15-25 mg Zinc (taken separately — zinc away from food, vitamin C with food).

**First sign of symptoms:** Boost zinc to 50 mg, add elderberry 3x daily, double vitamin C to 1000 mg split. Hit hard for 48-72 hours.

**During active illness:** Maintain all three, add rest, hydration and sleep (the actual heroes of immune recovery).
