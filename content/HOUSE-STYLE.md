# The Apothecary — house style for blog articles (used by humans AND the monthly article-writer workflow)

Voice: Precious, One Life Health Consultant, 30 years behind the Centurion
counter. Warm, direct, zero hype. We tell people NOT to buy things. Honest
observations from the counter beat clinical citations, but never overclaim:
supplements "support", they don't "cure" or "treat". POPIA- and
SAHPRA-conscious: no disease-treatment claims.

Brand facts (violations are bugs):
- Free delivery over R400 nationwide. Collect free in store.
- Stores: Centurion · Glen Village · Edenvale. Since 1996.
- "Health consultants" (never coaches, never pharmacists).
- 17 consultant-signed protocols. Real codes only: FIRST10 (10% off over
  R500), STACK5 (5% off 3+ items), STACK10 (10% off 5+ items),
  DISPENSARY10 (10% off, orders over R600).
- Sign-off: "— Precious".

Structure per article (700–1,000 words):
1. A counter anecdote or honest observation as the hook (2-3 sentences).
2. The plain-language mechanism (why the thing happens).
3. What we'd actually suggest — specific, dosed, honest about limits.
4. One "when NOT to bother" paragraph (the trust builder).
5. Close with the matching protocol or collection, soft CTA, WhatsApp line.

Frontmatter must match scripts/publish_blog.py exactly (title, slug, handle:
health-wellness-hub, excerpt, subject, preview, campaign_segment: S3MAsK,
send_offset_days: 1, products list with name/price/url/badge/blurb).
PRODUCTS MUST BE REAL AND IN STOCK — verify against the live store before
queueing; never invent a handle or price. SA context always: winter is
June-August, load-shedding-era habits, rand prices.
