# World-Class Premium D2C Supplement Website Teardowns
**Date:** 2026-07-02 · Research input for the Vivid Health dedicated Shopify store redesign (`vivid-health-redesign-blueprint-2026-07-02.md`). All sites fetched live 2026-07-02 unless noted; AG1 and Aesop block crawlers — their sections are reconstructed from secondary sources and flagged.

## 1. RITUAL (ritual.com) — The Traceability Benchmark

**Design language.** Clean white canvas, black type, soft pastel product accents. Sans-serif throughout, generous section spacing. Unusually **video-forward** homepage — looping demonstration videos rather than static lifestyle shots.

**Homepage order:** Promo banner → nav → video hero → Best Sellers grid → trust credentials strip → "Our radical idea" value-prop video → traceability video section → "Find your Ritual" category grid → 4 testimonials → **scientific leadership bios (5 profiles incl. a Cornell professor and Cedars-Sinai physician)** → "For Skeptics, By Skeptics" community carousel → categories → email capture → footer.

**PDP anatomy (Women's Multivitamin 18+)** — the most complete evidence-stack PDP in the category:
1. Gallery (5 images) + "Best Seller" badge
2. Four benefit callouts (Brain / Bone / Blood-Building / Antioxidants, each naming the responsible nutrient)
3. Price + subscription toggle ($26.40 sub vs $37.50 + $10.99 shipping one-time — shipping punishes one-time)
4. Subscription benefits block (30-day money-back, free shipping, cancel anytime)
5. Full Supplement Facts table
6. **Certification badge grid** (USP Verified, Clean Label Project, Non-GMO Project, ClimeCo, Vegan, Made Traceable®)
7. How to Use (incl. the "Signature Scent Tab" mint delight-detail)
8. **"Certificate of Traceability"**
9. **Traceable Ingredients carousel — 9 cards: ingredient form, final manufacturing location ("Pisticci, Italy"), source material, "found in" foods, "Get to Know Our Supplier" links**
10. Patented delivery tech explainer (delayed-release, beadlet-in-oil)
11. Cross-sell → FAQ → Scientific Advisory Board → Reviews (4.6★, 1,051, "90% would recommend")

**Signature features.** Made Traceable® supply chain cards with named suppliers; per-product environmental disclosure ("Carbon Footprint: 0.483kg CO2e", "100% post-consumer recycled PET"); the competitive certification flex: *"None of the five best-selling women's multivitamins are USP Verified, Non-GMO Project Verified, and disclose their clinical study information on their site."*

**Subscription.** 20% off single subs, 30% off 2+ bundles (new subscribers), free shipping always, cancel anytime, 30-day money-back.

**Trust devices.** "$5M Investment in Clinical Studies", "2.6 Billion Capsules Sold", university-led 12-week clinical trial, batch testing (heavy metals/microbes/10 allergens), B Corp.

**Voice:** "Our radical idea: supplements should work." / "*Clean* isn't clear enough. We're traceable." / "For Skeptics, By Skeptics."

## 2. SEED (seed.com) — Science-First Education

**Design language.** White/cream with earth tones and greens; quasi-clinical product photography (capsules dispensed, bathroom counters); Mux video throughout; card-based modularity; heavy whitespace.

**Homepage order:** Nav → hero ("A life-changing health routine, built for your microbiome.") → 4-product grid with per-month pricing → "Bundle + Save 25%" → **ViaCap® dual-capsule technology diagram** → "You are more than human" education ("home to 38 trillion microbes") → video testimonial ("Over 1 million health transformations") → press/UGC (Fast Company, Forbes) → SeedLabs research callout → final CTA.

**PDP anatomy (DS-01® Daily Synbiotic):** title + 13,562 reviews → value prop + price ($30 intro from $49.99; "30-day supply delivered monthly. Pause or cancel anytime.") → benefits + clinical trials link → ingredients → **ViaCap® explainer ("Unlike many probiotics, DS-01® makes it to the gut alive.")** → **24-strain formula in 4 benefit blends, dosed in AFU not CFU** → **8-badge allergen-testing grid** → comparison table vs other probiotics → sustainable delivery (refillable glass jar Welcome Kit) → testing standards → FAQ.

**Signature features.** Inventing its own precision units (AFU); sustainability-as-luxury (glass Welcome Kit, compostable refills); a research arm (SeedLabs) positioning the company as a science institution; "Microbiome 101".

**Subscription.** Effectively subscription-only; strong intro discount; "30 Day Money-Back Guarantee on First Order **without Product Return**."

**Voice:** "You are more than human." / "Whole body health starts in the gut."

## 3. AG1 / Athletic Greens (drinkag1.com) — One-Product Storytelling

## 10. AESOP (aesop.com) — The Premium Restraint Reference
*(Crawl-blocked; from Fonts In Use, Work & Co case study, design-analysis coverage.)*

**Design language.** **Optima (Zapf Humanist 601) display + Suisse Int'l UI**, Neue Helvetica packaging. Monochromatic muted amber/beige mirroring the bottles; **symmetrical product imagery on a shared baseline axis**; slow elegant transitions "that mirror a relaxing in-store experience."

**Homepage.** Opens with **a quiet video of lab-coated formulators — no promo banners, no urgency, no discounts anywhere**. Navigation by real-world use (travel, home, hand). Returning users get recommendations tuned by purchase history, **location and seasonal climate**.

**What to steal.** (1) One typeface pairing from label to site to store; (2) explain products through ingredients/texture/routine-fit — *"If a customer understands the ingredients, the texture, and the routine fit, the price feels like a trade, not a toll"*; (3) **restraint itself is the trust signal** — zero countdown timers, zero stacked promos.

### Strategic observations
1. **The premium spectrum runs Cymbiotika (loud) → Aesop (silent).** The profitable middle — Ritual/Wild Nutrition: calm design + one clear subscription offer + radical transparency — is where a SA-botanicals house brand belongs.
2. **Traceability is the most ownable pattern for Vivid** — the South African version (named SA manufacturer and botanical origins) done more authentically than any import competitor, using metafields and photography.
3. **Trust is modular and mostly cheap.** 12 of 20 patterns are copy, metafields and standard apps. The expensive ones came *after* the cheap ones were flawless.
