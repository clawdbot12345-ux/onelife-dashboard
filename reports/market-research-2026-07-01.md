# SA Health E-com Competitor Findings (2026-07-01)

**BLUF:** Competitive standard = free delivery at R400–R650; loyalty worth 1.5–2% back (or Dis-Chem 10–15% instant on selected brands). Onelife's R400 threshold is at parity with FTN/Wellness Warehouse and BETTER than Dis-Chem (R600), Clicks (R650), Takealot (R650). Differentiation = expertise/curation, subscriptions, same-day Gauteng fulfilment — not price.

## Onelife itself (verified on onelife.co.za)
- "South Africa's apothecary, since 1996", consultant-led, 10,000+ products, 250+ brands (Solgar, NOW, house brand Vivid Health, Natroceutics).
- Free delivery >R400 (1–5 days); same-day pickup 3 stores; free membership (10% off first order, points); **Subscribe & Save exists**; practitioner pricing; 17 Dispensary protocol stacks (DISPENSARY10); supplement quiz (5% off stack).

## Faithful to Nature (closest competitor; primary 403-blocked)
- Free shipping ≥R400 carbon-neutral; Pargo pickup; 2-hr express CPT. Loyalty points **expire after 6 months** (weakness to exploit). Referral R50; newsletter = R100 off R400+. Heavy voucher culture. **No Subscribe & Save found.**

## Wellness Warehouse (VERIFIED on primary)
- Free >R400; R60 under; WellnessNOW same-day express R35 (R100 min); **Click & Collect FREE all orders, same-day, 7 days**. Rewards programme exists (earn rate unknown). Monthly/quarterly catalogues.

## Dis-Chem (2025 loyalty overhaul — key intel)
- Delivery R35 under R600; FREE over R600; DeliverD 60-min via app.
- **Better Rewards (21 Oct 2025)**: replaced Benefit (1.5% back) with **10% instant discount on ~180 brands (~10k products), 15% with Capitec pay**. 9M members migrated; credited for market-share gains. Brand-selective → room on premium/practitioner brands.

## Clicks
- Delivery R80, FREE >R650; C&C R35, free >R350. ClubCard ~2% cashback base, double points after R1,000/2-mo cycle; cashback expires 12 months. Heavy 3-for-2 vitamins promos.

## Takealot
- Free delivery threshold raised to R650 (2025), fee R75. TakealotMORE R39/99 per month (unlimited delivery). Marketplace price competition on mainstream brands (USN, Biogen, NPL, Solal).

## Chrome SA
- Sports-nutrition specialist, 22 stores, free delivery (conditions unverified). No subscription programme found.

## Market size
- SA online pharmacy ~US$111m (2024), +20–25% growth 2025 (ECDB). Online = fastest-growing channel. No credible SA health e-com CVR/AOV benchmarks — global ~2–3% CVR proxy.

## Strategic takeaways
1. R400 free-shipping = at parity/better — market it, don't necessarily raise it blindly (but weigh against delivery cost problem).
2. Loyalty norm ~2% back; FTN 6-month expiry is exploitable.
3. **Nobody else verified runs a supplement autoship** — Subscribe & Save + practitioner pricing + same-day Gauteng pickup = clearest differentiators.
4. Category tailwind ~20%+ online growth.

# TikTok Commerce & Monetization in South Africa — Research Findings (2026-07-01)

## 1. TikTok Shop NOT live in SA — no confirmed African launch (HIGH confidence)
- Live in ~16–20 markets (US, UK, EU big-5 + Jun-2026 expansion AT/BE/NL/PL, SEA, MX, BR). No African country on any availability list.
- Only Africa signal: Kenya on one aggregator watchlist (unconfirmed, low confidence). TikTok Africa newsroom has no commerce announcement (checked 2026-07-01).
- GrowthPulse Media's "TikTok Shop South Africa 2026 Guide" claims it's live — SEO misinformation, contradicts all availability lists. Do not rely on it.
- TikTok Digital Commerce Labs education program expanding to Africa in 2026 (seller-education precursor, not a launch).
- Sources: socialtale.co/blog/tiktok-shop-countries-live-2026; dpl.company/countries-with-access-to-tiktok-shop-seller-center/; novadata.io (June 2026 EU); growthcharter.co.za (frames SA as "pre-launch").

## 2. Monetization paths available today (MED-HIGH)
- TikTok Ads fully live in SA (In-Feed, Spark Ads, Video Shopping Ads → external store). Shopify↔TikTok pixel/catalog integration works.
- Link-in-bio → Shopify: default organic path.
- **TikTok Live + WhatsApp checkout** — proven African pattern: TechCabal (Jan 2025) documents Nigerian sellers earning ~₦70k/day via Lives closed on WhatsApp/bank transfer. WhatsApp is SA's #1 favourite platform (34%), TikTok #2 (23.8%) — Meltwater 2025.
- Creator collabs + Spark Ads + discount-code attribution (no native affiliate program without Shop).

## 3. Benchmarks (LOW-MED — flag as indicative)
- SA in TikTok's low-cost CPM tier; emerging-market advertiser CPMs ~$1–4 vs $5–10 US. SA CPCs ~55% below US (WordStream). Global TikTok CPC $0.20–2.00.
- TikTok e-com CTR ~0.5–1.5%, CVR ~0.5–1.5% (below Meta/Google CVR but cheaper reach, stronger discovery). No SA-specific TikTok CVR data exists publicly.
- Link-out funnels leak at app-exit; WhatsApp-close funnels outperform in African markets.

## 4. SA case studies (LOW for named cases; HIGH for regional pattern)
- No documented named SA brand case with audited revenue. Pattern: demo-led short video → pinned comment/bio link for considered purchases; TikTok Live + WhatsApp close for impulse/drops; creator seeding with codes.

## 5. SA user base (HIGH — DataReportal Digital 2026)
- 29.1M users 18+ = 64.8% of SA adults; ad reach +33.2% during 2025. Adult ad audience 60.7% male / 39.3% female (atypical — flag). Usage skews 15–24. SA users avg 3h36m/day on social.

## Strategic takeaway
Monetize the follower lead now via: (1) Spark Ads + Shopify pixel website-conversion campaigns at SA's low CPMs; (2) TikTok Live + WhatsApp order-close funnel; (3) creator seeding with code attribution. Build catalog/live-selling muscle to be first in queue if Shop opens in SA.

# WhatsApp Commerce in South Africa 2025–2026 — Research Findings (2026-07-01)

## 1. Penetration (HIGH)
- ~94–96% of SA internet users use WhatsApp — #1 platform. ~26–28M users by 2026. Avg ~23h42m/month in-app; ~1 in 3 name it favourite app.

## 2. Payments status (HIGH — verified)
- **Native WhatsApp Pay NOT live in SA** (only India; Singapore business; Brazil in-chat card ended Jan 2026).
- SA reality = **payment links / hosted checkout in chat**: Clickatell Chat 2 Pay (live since 2023, Telkom flagship), Nedbank Money Message (2021), WigWag/Stitch bot (2025). Plan around Payfast/Yoco/SnapScan/Ozow links in chat.
- WhatsApp Business app + Catalogs fully available in SA.

## 3. What SA retailers do (HIGH/MED)
- Pick n Pay: WhatsApp self-service via Clickatell (catalogue, Smart Shopper loyalty, locator) for ~9M members — service/loyalty, not full checkout.
- TFG: account applications/balance checks. Telkom: full pay-in-chat (flagship case).
- No major SA grocer does full WhatsApp checkout yet (MED-HIGH). No public revenue case-study numbers — all conversion stats trace to vendors.

## 4. Benchmarks & pricing
- Vendor claims: ~98% open, 15–60% CTR — inflated; realistic planning: **opens 70–90%, CTR 10–25% for warm opted-in list**. "WhatsApp materially beats email/SMS on visibility" = HIGH confidence.
- **Meta per-message pricing since 1 Jul 2025** (conversation pricing deprecated). SA rate card: Marketing ≈ $0.0379 (~R0.65–0.70)/delivered msg; Utility ≈ $0.0076 (~R0.14); free-form replies in 24-h service window free. BSPs (Wati, 360dialog, Twilio, Infobip; SA: Chat Inc, Cue) add fees.
- 1,000-person VIP broadcast ≈ R650–700 Meta fees per marketing send.

## 5. Groups vs Channels vs broadcast vs API
- Group (current VIP): members see each other's numbers = **POPIA exposure**; ~1,024 cap; no analytics.
- Broadcast list (free app): 256/list, only reaches people who saved your number; manual.
- Channel: one-way, no personalisation/commerce.
- **Business API via BSP = the commerce path**: opted-in segmented broadcasts, catalog messages, in-chat cart, click-to-WhatsApp ads (free 72-h window), analytics. Template pre-approval; Meta quality rating throttles spam.
- Recommended flow: catalog/product message → in-chat cart → payment link (Payfast/Yoco/SnapScan/Ozow) → free utility confirmation.

## 6. POPIA (HIGH — important)
- Regulations amended effective 17 Apr 2025: direct e-marketing consent must be **express opt-in** ("opt-out shall not constitute consent"), prescribed consent forms; objection via any channel incl. WhatsApp. Existing-customer exception (s69) is narrow.
- Practical: record explicit WhatsApp-marketing consent per contact; STOP opt-out in every broadcast; **don't run marketing out of a group that exposes members' numbers**.

## Gaps
- No vendor-independent engagement benchmarks; re-verify Meta SA rate card at implementation.

# Google Ads Benchmarks & Structure — SA supplements e-com ~R8k/mo (2026-07-01)

## 1. Benchmarks
- SA search CPC avg R4–6 (Health R3.90); SA CTR 9.1% (Health 10.4%) — Adbot 2025, 1,200+ real SA campaigns.
- At R8k/mo and R4–8 CPC → ~1,000–1,800 clicks → at 1.5–3% CVR = ~15–50 orders/mo (boundary of Smart Bidding viability).
- Global e-com ROAS: median blended ~2.87x (2025, Upcounting); **Health & Wellness ~2.1x** (worst-declining vertical); Google avg ~3.68x vs Meta 1.93x (Eightx 2026); supplements ~2.3:1 (rule1). Treat 2–4x as normal band; **4x+ usually = branded-traffic contamination**.
- SA supplements CPA target: R80–250 depending on AOV (derived).

## 2. PMax brand cannibalization (HIGH confidence)
- Optmyzr study (Jul 2025, 503 accounts): 91% of accounts had PMax/Search keyword overlap; 97% search-term overlap; PMax wins branded queries even with dedicated exact-match brand campaigns; Search usually outperforms PMax on same terms.
- smec State of PMax 2025: brand share inside PMax materially inflates reported ROAS; publishes measuring script.
- **Mike Rhodes' free PMax script** (mikerhodes.com.au/scripts/pmax) buckets PMax search terms brand/near-brand/non-brand — the standard diagnostic; run it on the account = the audit's smoking gun.
- Fixes exist since 2023–2025: PMax **brand exclusion lists** (Google Ads Help 13721847, 14505308), campaign-level negative keywords in PMax (10,000 limit since Mar 2025).
- Recommended structure: (1) dedicated brand Search campaign, exact/phrase, capped budget; (2) PMax with brand exclusions so ROAS = incremental non-brand only; (3) run Rhodes script first to quantify.

## 3. Is PMax right at R8k/mo?
- Smart Bidding/PMax wants ~30 conversions/30 days per campaign. Below ~$1k/mo practitioners say avoid PMax (wastes on Display/YouTube, never exits learning) → prefer Standard Shopping + brand Search, ONE consolidated campaign.
- SA nuance: R8k buys ~$3k-equivalent click volume; if genuinely 30+ Google orders/mo, PMax-with-brand-excluded defensible; if 10–20, Standard Shopping + exact brand Search safer; tROAS only once volume supports.

## 4. Agency fees
- SA norm: **10–20% of spend, minimum retainers R2,500–7,500/mo** (converges across 4+ agency pricing guides; no independent survey).
- R5,500 on R14,000 = 39% — inside absolute minimum-retainer band (3 platforms) but 2–4x the % norm; ≥30% of spend = widely treated as economics stop making sense. Options: consolidate to 1–2 platforms, raise spend so fee ≤20–25%, or self-serve/lighter-touch.
- Each platform currently gets ~R4.7k — all below any learning threshold.

## Gaps
- No public SA e-com CVR/ROAS benchmark; Varos supplements median unretrievable; agency-fee norms self-reported.

# SA E-com Delivery Costs & Strategies (2026-07-01) — condensed

## Per-parcel costs
- Courier Guy: local overnight ~R65 (5kg); national overnight ~R130 (2kg) excl VAT.
- PUDO→"Lockers" (rebranded 2025; 3rd price hike Oct 2025): locker-to-locker ~R50–60 (cheapest); locker-to-door R69–R209; kiosk-to-door up to R250.
- Aramex Store-to-Door sleeve: R99.99 flat nationwide (PnP/FreshStop/Checkers).
- Pargo pickup point: ~R62 (2023, stale; expect R70–85); ~20% cheaper than door; 4,000+ points; Shopify app.
- Paxi (PEP): R59.95 flat satchel <5kg metro-metro — cheapest small-parcel.
- Bob Go (ex-uAfrica): dominant SA multi-courier Shopify app; free PAYG plan; paid R249–1,999/mo with discounted rates; rate-shopping across ~10 couriers.

## Benchmarks
- Customer-facing fees norm: R65–180 standard; volume/business economy R45–120/parcel.
- Free-shipping threshold: R500 = SA convention (Amazon SA R500; range R450–650).
- Delivery cost % of online revenue: global good practice ~6–10%. Onelife at ~14–15% (R17–20k on R130k) = clearly above norm. Shifting half of orders to locker/Pargo (~R60–85) or free store collection would plausibly bring under 10%.

## Market context
- SA online retail R96bn 2024 (+35%); >R130bn forecast 2025 (~10% of retail). Sixty60 = store-as-fulfilment-node benchmark.
- Clicks/Dis-Chem run free click-and-collect — the health-retail template. For 3-store Gauteng chain: free collect at stores (zero marginal cost), lockers/Pargo default cheap option, door-to-door as paid premium.

# Email/SMS/WhatsApp Benchmarks — Health & Wellness e-com (2026-07-01) — condensed

## Klaviyo Health & Beauty benchmarks (2026 official, HIGH)
- Campaign: open 30.5% (top10% 45%), click 1.24% (top 3.4%), placed-order 0.19%.
- Flow: click 4.8% (top ~10%), placed-order 1.96%.
- Campaign RPR avg ~$0.10, top ~$0.97. Flows = 5.3% of sends but ~41% of email revenue; RPR ~18× campaigns.
- Per-flow RPR (all-industry): Abandoned cart $3.07/$27.12 (conv 2.7–3.3% avg, 7.7% top); Welcome $2.35/$20.92 (conv ~2%/10%); Browse $0.95; Post-purchase $0.38. No official winback benchmark (community: weakest flow).
- Open rates inflated post-iOS15 — judge on clicks/RPR/placed orders.

## Email share of revenue
- "25–30%" claim = Klaviyo's own attribution on 2016 data. Realistic: <$1M brands 15–25% attributed; growth 25–35%; top decile email+SMS 38–45%. Onelife target: 20–30% (R26–39k/mo); 15–20% realistic near-term. (Currently 18% — already near band.)

## SMS vs WhatsApp SA
- Bulk SMS SA ~R0.20–0.35/msg. WhatsApp marketing ~R0.68/delivered; utility ~R0.14; replies in 24h window free.
- **Klaviyo launched native WhatsApp channel Sept 2025** (HIGH) — natural SA mobile channel from existing stack. ⚠️ Verify Klaviyo SMS supports SA numbers (historically not — likely SMS not available; WhatsApp is the path).
- Klaviyo: SMS over-indexes in health & beauty; SMS flows = 45% of SMS revenue.
- POPIA s69: prior opt-in for ALL electronic direct marketing incl WhatsApp (2024 Regulator guidance).

## List growth / recovery
- Popup submit rate: 3% = Klaviyo's honest bar; 2.4% with discount vs 1.7% without; single-field ~4.3%.
- Cart abandonment 70.2% global avg (Baymard, HIGH); email recovers 3–5% of abandoners.
